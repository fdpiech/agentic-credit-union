# Runbook: Community Disaster Response

> **Mode**: CANVAS-Full | **Duration**: Hours 0-72 (crisis) + Days 4-30 (stabilization) + Days 31-90+ (recovery) | **Agents**: 10 | **Lead**: CEO (macro) + Financial Advisor (micro case template)

> **Pattern**: Macro/Micro Split — A single runbook drives both an enterprise-level credit union response AND spawns individual member hardship case workflows at scale. The macro track manages institutional response; the micro track is a templated per-member workflow that repeats for each affected member.

---

## Scenario

A natural disaster — hurricane, tornado, flood, wildfire, earthquake, or severe winter storm — has struck the credit union's service area. Members are displaced, branches may be damaged or inaccessible, and the credit union must simultaneously manage its own operational continuity while delivering financial relief to affected members at scale. This runbook coordinates the enterprise-level disaster response (macro track) and provides a repeatable per-member hardship case template (micro track) that is instantiated for each affected member requesting assistance.

## When to Activate

- Federal or state disaster declaration covering the credit union's service area
- FEMA disaster declaration (Individual Assistance or Public Assistance)
- NCUA disaster guidance letter issued for the affected area
- Significant local disaster affecting 50+ members or any branch location
- Governor or local executive emergency declaration in the credit union's counties
- Credit union branch or facility damaged or rendered inaccessible by disaster

## Regulatory Framework

| Obligation | Regulation | Owner | Deadline |
|-----------|-----------|-------|---------|
| NCUA disaster notification — operational status | NCUA guidance letters (disaster-specific) | Compliance Officer | Within 24 hours of branch closure or significant operational impact |
| NCUA flexible examination expectations | NCUA disaster guidance letters | Compliance Officer | Document accommodations; maintain for next examination |
| Reg E timing accommodations — error resolution | 12 CFR 1005.11 | Deposit Operations Manager + Compliance Officer | NCUA typically grants timing extensions via guidance letter — document reliance |
| FEMA coordination — disaster loan referrals | FEMA Individual Assistance program | Financial Advisor | Ongoing during disaster recovery period |
| BSA/AML — disaster-period transaction monitoring | FinCEN guidance on disaster-related activity | BSA Officer (support role) | Ongoing — adjust monitoring for disaster-period patterns |
| Fair lending — disaster relief must be equitable | 12 CFR 202 (Reg B) | Compliance Officer | All disaster relief applied consistently regardless of protected class |
| Call report adjustments — disaster-related losses | NCUA Call Report instructions | CFO (support role) | Next quarterly call report filing |

> **Critical**: NCUA routinely issues disaster-specific guidance letters granting regulatory flexibility — extended examination timelines, filing extensions, and encouragement to work with affected members. The Compliance Officer must obtain and document reliance on the specific guidance letter for this disaster.

## MACRO TRACK: Enterprise Disaster Response

### Hours 0-24: Crisis Response

```
Activate CEO — DISASTER DECLARED.

Disaster type: [Hurricane / Tornado / Flood / Wildfire / Earthquake / Winter storm]
FEMA declaration number: [If issued — or "Pending"]
Affected area: [Counties / cities / zip codes]
Branch impact: [List branches affected — open / closed / damaged / destroyed]
Estimated members in affected area: [Number]
Staff impact: [Known staff displacement or injury]

IMMEDIATE ACTIONS (Hours 0-6):
1. Activate disaster response protocol — all macro track agents on alert
2. Branch status assessment:
   - Which branches are operational?
   - Which branches must close? (safety, access, power, structural damage)
   - Alternate service locations available? (shared branching, partner CU, mobile branch)
3. Staff welfare check — account for all employees in affected area
4. Notify NCUA regional office — credit union operational status
5. Activate business continuity plan — core system access from alternate locations
6. Board Chair notification — verbal briefing; written update within 24 hours

Activate Branch Manager — BRANCH OPERATIONS CONTINUITY.

Branch operations (Hours 0-24):
1. Physical assessment of each branch in affected area — safe to operate?
2. Branches that must close:
   - Member notification — signage, website, phone system message
   - Redirect members to nearest open branch or shared branching location
   - ATM status — operational, powered, accessible, cash levels
3. Branches that can operate:
   - Extended hours if member demand warrants
   - Cash management — increased cash needs for disaster-affected members
   - Generator power if utility power is out
4. Mobile branch or temporary service location deployment (if available)
5. Report to CEO: branch status summary, estimated reopening timeline

Activate Core Systems Administrator — SYSTEM CONTINUITY.

System actions (Hours 0-12):
1. Core system operational status — accessible from all functioning locations
2. Online banking and mobile app operational — members need remote access
3. ATM network monitoring — identify offline ATMs, coordinate cash replenishment
4. Remote access for staff displaced from normal work locations
5. Temporary connectivity solutions for branches operating on generator or alternate sites
6. Report to CEO: all systems operational / issues identified and mitigation plan

Activate CHRO — STAFF WELFARE AND DEPLOYMENT.

Staff actions (Hours 0-24):
1. Account for all employees in affected area — personal safety and housing status
2. Identify staff who cannot report to their normal branch — reassignment options
3. Emergency staffing plan for branches with increased demand
4. Employee assistance program activation for affected staff
5. Volunteer coordination — staff from unaffected areas to support affected branches
6. Report to CEO: staff status, staffing gaps, deployment plan
```

### Hours 24-72: Stabilization and Communication

```
Activate Marketing Manager — DISASTER MEMBER COMMUNICATIONS.

Communication actions (Hours 24-48):
1. Member-wide disaster communication:
   - Branch status — open, closed, alternate locations
   - How to access accounts — online, mobile, ATM, shared branching
   - Disaster relief programs available — loan mods, fee waivers, emergency loans
   - How to request assistance — phone number, email, branch visit
   - FEMA registration information and referral
2. Website disaster information page — updated daily during crisis period
3. Social media — service updates, community support messaging
4. Local media coordination (CEO approval required for all media statements)
5. Community partnership announcements — Red Cross, United Way, local relief efforts

Activate Compliance Officer — REGULATORY ACCOMMODATION DOCUMENTATION.

Regulatory actions (Hours 24-72):
1. Obtain NCUA disaster guidance letter for this specific disaster event
2. Document all regulatory accommodations the credit union is relying on:
   - Reg E error resolution timing extensions
   - Examination timeline flexibility
   - Filing deadline extensions (call reports, HMDA, other)
3. Prepare disaster accommodation policy memo for board approval:
   - Loan modification authority and parameters
   - Fee waiver authority and scope
   - Emergency lending parameters
   - Skip-a-pay program activation
4. Fair lending documentation: disaster relief applied equitably across all member segments
5. Report to CEO: regulatory framework established for disaster response
```

### Days 4-30: Member Relief Operations (Macro Coordination)

```
Activate CEO — DISASTER RELIEF PROGRAM ACTIVATION.

Program activation (Day 4):
1. Board approval (emergency session or written consent):
   - Disaster loan modification program — terms and authority limits
   - Emergency loan program — terms, amounts, eligibility
   - Fee waiver program — scope and duration
   - Skip-a-pay program — eligible loan types, maximum skips
   - Early CD withdrawal penalty waiver — scope and duration
2. FEMA coordination — register as community partner for financial counseling referrals
3. Community presence plan — CEO visible in community recovery efforts
4. Budget allocation for disaster relief — loan loss reserve impact, fee income reduction
5. Weekly board updates during active relief period

CEO ongoing (Days 4-30):
1. Daily situation reports from all macro track agents
2. Weekly board briefing — member impact, relief program utilization, financial impact
3. NCUA communication — ongoing operational updates as requested
4. Community presence — attend local recovery meetings, coordinate with civic leaders
5. Monitor staff morale — disaster response is exhausting; rotate staff; provide support
```

## MICRO TRACK: Per-Member Hardship Case Template

> This template is instantiated once for each affected member who requests disaster assistance. The Financial Advisor leads each case through intake, assessment, and relief plan execution.

### Step 1: Member Intake

```
Activate Financial Advisor — MEMBER DISASTER HARDSHIP INTAKE.

Member: [Name / Account Number]
Disaster impact: [Property damage / Displacement / Job loss / Injury / Business loss]
FEMA registration: [Registered — FEMA # / Not yet registered / Not eligible]
Current status: [Evacuated / Sheltering in place / Temporary housing / Returned home]

Intake assessment:
1. Immediate financial needs:
   - [ ] Unable to make loan payment(s) — which loans?
   - [ ] Needs emergency cash — amount and purpose
   - [ ] Fees causing hardship — which fees?
   - [ ] Needs early access to CD funds without penalty
   - [ ] Insurance claim filed? Status?
   - [ ] FEMA assistance applied for? Status?

2. Income disruption:
   - [ ] Employed — can return to work? Timeline?
   - [ ] Self-employed — business impact?
   - [ ] Receiving disaster unemployment assistance?
   - [ ] Other income sources affected?

3. Housing status:
   - [ ] Home habitable?
   - [ ] Insurance coverage — homeowner / renter?
   - [ ] Temporary housing needs and costs?

Financial Advisor assessment: Document member situation and recommend relief package
```

### Step 2: Relief Plan Design

```
Activate Financial Advisor — DESIGN MEMBER RELIEF PLAN.

Based on intake assessment, activate applicable relief components:

LOAN RELIEF (activate Loan Officer + Collections Specialist):
- [ ] Skip-a-pay: Loan(s) [list] — skip [1-3] payments
      → Activate Loan Officer to process skip-a-pay
- [ ] Loan modification: Loan(s) [list] — [reduced payment / extended term / rate reduction / forbearance]
      → Activate Loan Officer to process modification
- [ ] Payment deferral: Move [X] months of payments to end of loan term
      → Activate Loan Officer to process deferral
- [ ] Collection suspension: Account(s) [list] — suspend collections activity for [30/60/90] days
      → Activate Collections Specialist to suspend and code accounts

EMERGENCY LENDING (activate Loan Officer):
- [ ] Emergency personal loan: $[amount] at [disaster relief rate] for [term]
      → Activate Loan Officer to originate emergency loan
- [ ] Emergency line of credit: $[amount] for disaster recovery expenses
      → Activate Loan Officer to originate

DEPOSIT RELIEF (activate Deposit Operations Manager):
- [ ] Fee waivers: [Overdraft / NSF / ATM / Wire / Other] fees waived for [30/60/90] days
      → Activate Deposit Operations Manager to apply fee waivers
- [ ] Early CD withdrawal: CD account(s) [list] — penalty waived
      → Activate Deposit Operations Manager to process penalty-free withdrawal
- [ ] Temporary overdraft protection increase: $[amount] for [30/60/90] days
      → Activate Deposit Operations Manager to adjust

REFERRALS:
- [ ] FEMA Individual Assistance: fema.gov or 1-800-621-3362
- [ ] SBA disaster loans (business or home): sba.gov/disaster
- [ ] State emergency assistance programs: [state-specific resources]
- [ ] Insurance claim assistance: [if applicable]
- [ ] HUD housing counseling: [if housing displacement]

Financial Advisor: Document complete relief plan — member acknowledgment obtained
```

### Step 3: Relief Plan Execution and Follow-Up

```
Activate Financial Advisor — EXECUTE AND MONITOR MEMBER RELIEF PLAN.

Execution tracking:
- [ ] All approved relief components processed by respective agents
- [ ] Member confirmation sent — summary of all accommodations in effect
- [ ] Follow-up date set: [30 days from intake] — reassess member situation
- [ ] Case documented in member file — disaster hardship code applied

30-day follow-up:
1. Contact member — has situation improved, stabilized, or worsened?
2. Are current accommodations sufficient or do they need extension?
3. Is member on track to resume normal payments?
4. FEMA/insurance claim status — has member received assistance?
5. Extend, modify, or close hardship case based on assessment

60-day follow-up (if case still open):
1. Reassess all accommodations — extend or begin transition to normal terms
2. Financial wellness plan — budget adjustment for recovery period
3. Update case documentation

90-day close-out:
1. Final assessment — member returned to normal financial footing?
2. All temporary accommodations expired or transitioned to permanent modification
3. Case closed with full documentation
4. Referral to ongoing Financial Wellness program (Workflow P) if appropriate
```

**Gate Keepers**: CEO (enterprise response) + Financial Advisor (member case quality) + Compliance Officer (regulatory documentation)

**Gate Decision**:
- **CRISIS**: Active disaster response — all macro track agents fully engaged; micro track intake active
- **STABILIZED**: Branches operational; relief programs running; new intake declining
- **RECOVERY**: Member cases in follow-up; operations normalized; lessons learned documented
- **CLOSED**: All member cases resolved or transitioned; board final report delivered; examination file complete

## Key Risks to Manage

| Risk | Owner | Mitigation |
|------|-------|-----------|
| Branch inaccessible — members cannot access funds | Branch Manager + Core Systems Administrator | Shared branching, mobile branch, ATM cash, online/mobile banking promoted |
| Regulatory accommodation not documented — examination exposure | Compliance Officer | Document every accommodation with NCUA guidance letter citation |
| Disaster relief applied inconsistently — fair lending risk | Compliance Officer + Financial Advisor | Standardized intake template; relief criteria applied uniformly |
| Staff burnout during extended disaster response | CHRO | Rotate staff; mandatory rest days; EAP resources; recognize effort |
| Loan loss reserve inadequate for disaster-related losses | CFO | Early loss estimation; board-approved reserve adjustment; NCUA communication |
| Member fraud exploiting disaster relief programs | Financial Advisor + Loan Officer | Verify member address in disaster area; standard underwriting on emergency loans |
| FEMA/insurance proceeds not applied to CU loans | Loan Officer + Collections Specialist | Loan agreements may require insurance proceeds applied to loan balance — verify and communicate |
