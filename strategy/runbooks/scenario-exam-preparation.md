# Runbook: NCUA Examination Preparation

> **Mode**: CANVAS-Full | **Duration**: 12-16 weeks | **Agents**: All | **Lead**: Compliance Officer

---

## Scenario

The credit union is entering its NCUA examination cycle. An examiner is scheduled to arrive in 12-16 weeks for a combined Safety & Soundness, Consumer Compliance, and BSA examination. The goal is clean results — zero Material Recommendations for Action (MRAs) on CMS-covered areas — achieved through proactive preparation, not reactive scrambling.

## When to Activate

- NCUA examination notice received (typically 2-4 weeks before arrival)
- Annual exam preparation cycle (Q3 each year regardless of notice)
- State regulatory examination notice received
- Internal audit findings that require remediation before next exam
- Prior examination MRA that must be closed before follow-up visit

## Exam Preparation Timeline

### Phase 1: Gap Assessment (Weeks 1-4)

```
WEEK 1: Situation Assessment
├── Compliance Officer: Pull prior examination report; catalog all MRAs and recommendations
├── Compliance Officer: Run gap assessment against current state for each prior finding
├── Internal Auditor: Launch pre-exam audit of highest-risk areas identified in prior exam
├── BSA Officer: BSA program self-assessment against FFIEC BSA/AML Manual
└── Compliance Officer: Distribute document request list to all functional leads

WEEK 2: Regulatory Change Review
├── Compliance Officer: Identify all regulatory changes since last examination
├── Compliance Officer: Assess implementation status for each change (complete / in progress / not started)
├── Core Systems Administrator: Review NCUA Part 748 / GLBA Safeguards annual risk assessment status
├── BSA Officer: Review FinCEN guidance updates; assess program currency
└── Compliance Officer: Flag any implementation gaps to CEO

WEEK 3: Testing Launch
├── Compliance Officer: TILA/Reg Z — sample 25 consumer loan files; test disclosure accuracy
├── Compliance Officer: Reg B/ECOA — review adverse action notices for timing and reason codes
├── Compliance Officer: HMDA — reconcile LAR against application log; verify data accuracy
├── BSA Officer: CIP testing — sample 25 new accounts; verify all 4 elements documented
└── Internal Auditor: Commence risk-based audit program (lending, deposits, BSA)

WEEK 4: Testing Completion and Gap Identification
├── Compliance Officer: Complete all testing; document findings with regulatory citations
├── Internal Auditor: Complete audit fieldwork; draft findings
├── Compliance Officer: Compile master finding list — prior MRAs + new testing findings
└── Compliance Officer: Present gap assessment to CEO — remediation plan developed
```

### Phase 2: Remediation (Weeks 5-10)

```
WEEK 5-6: High-Priority Remediation
├── Each functional agent: Address assigned findings per compliance officer gap list
├── Loan Officer: Correct any identified TILA/adverse action issues; retrain on protocols
├── Mortgage Loan Processor: Address any TRID timing issues; update procedures
├── BSA Officer: Close any CIP gaps; update SAR/CTR documentation
├── Deposit Operations Manager: Address Reg E, Reg CC, or Truth in Savings findings
└── Compliance Officer: Track remediation progress weekly; escalate stalls to CEO

WEEK 7-8: Documentation Build
├── Compliance Officer: Assemble or update all program policies for examiner review
├── Compliance Officer: Compile compliance testing reports with management response
├── BSA Officer: Compile BSA program documentation (policy, procedures, training, testing)
├── Core Systems Administrator: Compile information security risk assessment and board approval
├── CHRO: Compile compliance training completion records for all staff
└── Internal Auditor: Issue draft audit report; obtain management responses

WEEK 9-10: Pre-Exam Mock Review
├── Compliance Officer: Conduct internal "mock exam" on highest-risk areas
├── Internal Auditor: Validate remediation evidence for all closed prior MRAs
├── Compliance Officer: Verify all document request items are assembled and indexed
├── CEO: Board compliance briefing — examination scope, known risks, management confidence level
└── All agents: Final staff training on examination protocols (cooperate, don't speculate)
```

### Phase 3: Examination Execution (Weeks 11-14)

```
PRE-ARRIVAL (Week 11)
├── Compliance Officer: Final document package prepared and organized
├── Compliance Officer: Secure examination workspace prepared
├── All agents: Briefed on examiner protocols — responsive, accurate, timely
└── CEO: Examiner welcome plan prepared

EXAMINATION ON-SITE (Weeks 11-14)
├── Compliance Officer: Manage all examiner document requests — 24-hour turnaround SLA
├── Each functional agent: Support examiner interviews in their area
├── Compliance Officer: Track all examiner questions and requests in log
├── CEO: Daily debrief with Compliance Officer on examination developments
├── Internal Auditor: Provide independent documentation to examiners on request
└── All agents: No speculation — "I'll confirm that and get back to you" is always acceptable

EXIT MEETING (Week 14)
├── CEO: Attend exit meeting with Compliance Officer
├── Compliance Officer: Document all verbal findings from exit meeting
├── CEO: Professional, constructive response to verbal findings
└── Compliance Officer: Begin drafting written response immediately
```

### Phase 4: Response and Close (Weeks 15-16)

```
WEEK 15: Draft Examination Response
├── Compliance Officer: Draft management responses to all MRAs and recommendations
├── Each functional agent: Review and validate management response language in their area
├── Risk Manager: Validate any capital or risk-related response commitments
├── CEO: Review and finalize all management responses
└── CEO: Prepare board examination presentation

WEEK 16: Board Presentation and Filing
├── CEO: Present examination report and management response to board
├── Board: Vote to accept examination report and management response
├── CEO: File management response with NCUA per required timeline
├── Compliance Officer: Implement corrective action plans per response commitments
└── Compliance Officer: Schedule follow-up tracking for all open commitments
```

## Quality Gate Checklist

| # | Criterion | Owner | Status |
|---|-----------|-------|--------|
| 1 | All prior MRAs closed with documented evidence | Compliance Officer | ☐ |
| 2 | Consumer compliance testing completed — all planned tests | Compliance Officer | ☐ |
| 3 | BSA independent testing complete and documented | Internal Auditor / BSA Officer | ☐ |
| 4 | All document request items assembled and indexed | Compliance Officer | ☐ |
| 5 | Staff compliance training at 100% completion | CHRO | ☐ |
| 6 | Board briefed on examination scope and status | CEO | ☐ |
| 7 | Information security risk assessment board-approved | Core Systems Administrator | ☐ |
| 8 | HMDA LAR reconciled and accurate | Compliance Officer | ☐ |
| 9 | Adverse action notices verified — timing and content | Compliance Officer | ☐ |
| 10 | BSA program policy board-approved — current year | BSA Officer | ☐ |

**Gate Keeper**: Compliance Officer + Internal Auditor (independent)

**Gate Decision**:
- **EXAM-READY**: All criteria met — proceed to examination with confidence
- **CONDITIONAL**: Minor gaps remain — address within 5 business days; document plan
- **NOT READY**: Material gaps present — CEO notified; remediation before examiner arrives

---

## Key Decision Points

| Decision | Timing | Authority | Criteria |
|----------|--------|-----------|---------|
| Voluntary disclosure to NCUA (if material violation found) | Before examiner arrives | CEO + Legal Counsel | Violation is curable; self-disclosure reduces penalty risk |
| Request examination delay | Upon receipt of notice | CEO | Only if significant remediation in progress; rare |
| Engage outside compliance counsel | Week 1 | CEO | Prior MRA unresolved; known significant exposure |
| Accept vs. contest examination finding | Week 15 | CEO | Factual basis for contesting; legal counsel review |
| Board response to critical finding | Week 16 | Board | Material issue affecting safety and soundness |

## Common Exam Preparation Pitfalls

| Pitfall | Prevention |
|---------|-----------|
| Assembling documents reactively — scrambling when examiner arrives | Phase 1 document assembly; request list sent Week 1 |
| Staff unable to explain their own compliance processes | CHRO coordinates training before exam — not during |
| Prior MRA resurfacing because remediation was superficial | Internal Auditor validates corrective action — not just Compliance Officer self-assessment |
| Speculation or over-explanation to examiners | All-staff exam protocol training: answer the question, stop |
| BSA program not independently tested | Annual BSA testing is mandatory — start in Q1, not Q3 |
| HMDA data errors discovered during examination | HMDA reconciliation in Q2; LAR reviewed before submission |
