## 1. Strategic Foundation

### 1.1 What CANVAS Solves

Credit union operations span dozens of interconnected processes — lending, deposits, compliance, member service, collections, and strategic planning. Without coordination, agents produce:
- Inconsistent member experiences across touchpoints
- Compliance gaps at process handoff boundaries
- Duplicated effort between lending, deposits, and service teams
- No shared regulatory context or institutional memory
- Decisions that optimize one function at the expense of another

**CANVAS eliminates these failure modes** by defining:
- **Who** activates at each workflow stage
- **What** they produce and for whom
- **When** they hand off and to whom
- **How** quality and compliance are verified before advancement
- **Why** each agent exists in the workflow (no passengers)

### 1.2 Core Principles

| Principle | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Member-First** | Every process decision is evaluated by its impact on member financial wellbeing |
| **Compliance Continuity** | No workflow advances through a regulated step without compliance verification |
| **Context Preservation** | Every handoff carries full member history — no agent starts cold |
| **Parallel Execution** | Independent workstreams run concurrently to compress cycle times |
| **Evidence Over Assertion** | All quality and compliance assessments require documentation, not verbal confirmation |
| **Cooperative Values** | The credit union difference is people helping people — every interaction reflects it |

### 1.3 The Agent Roster by Function

CANVAS coordinates **35 specialist agents** across 12 functional areas. The C-suite sets direction; functional leads execute workflows; risk, compliance, and audit gate every regulated step.

| Function | Agents | Primary CANVAS Role |
|----------|--------|---------------------|
| **Executive / C-Suite** | CEO, CFO, CHRO, COO, CTO, CIO | Strategic direction, financial governance, talent, operations, technology, and information strategy |
| **Lending** | Loan Officer, Business Lending Officer, Mortgage Loan Processor, Loan Underwriting Analyst | Originate, underwrite, and close consumer, business, and real estate loans |
| **Member Services** | Member Services Representative, Financial Advisor | Onboard members, service accounts, deliver financial guidance and wellness coaching, resolve complex cross-functional issues |
| **Operations** | Deposit Operations Manager, Card Services Specialist | Manage deposit products, card programs, and back-office operations |
| **Risk & Compliance** | Compliance Officer, Risk Manager, BSA Officer, Fraud Detection Analyst, Regulatory Affairs Analyst | Regulatory compliance, enterprise risk, anti-money laundering, fraud detection, and regulatory horizon scanning |
| **Collections** | Collections Specialist | Delinquency management, workouts, recovery strategy, and portfolio loss mitigation |
| **Branch** | Branch Manager | Front-line member experience, branch operations, and team leadership |
| **Marketing & Strategy** | Marketing Manager, Competitive Intelligence Analyst | Member growth, product promotion, community presence, and competitive/market intelligence |
| **Audit** | Internal Auditor | Independent assurance, exam support, and governance |
| **Technology** | Core Systems Administrator, IT Infrastructure Engineer, Database Administrator, Software Engineer, IT Services Manager, IT Systems Administrator, RPA & Automation Engineer, Business Intelligence Analyst | Systems reliability, security, automation, data, and technology operations |

> **Coordination research**: Multi-agent cooperation patterns (trust scoring, Shapley value attribution) used to design CANVAS handoffs are documented in `agentic-credit-union/research/game-theory.md`.

           ├──▶ Loan Origination (B) ──▶ Mortgage Processing (C)
           │              │

           │
           ├──▶ Deposit Operations (E) ──▶ Fraud / Dispute (H) [if Reg E claim]
           │              │
           │              └──▶ Card Services (I) [if card channel]
           │
           └──▶ Financial Advisor / Financial Wellness (P) [life events, hardship, goals]

                                   Regulatory Affairs Analyst feeds horizon to F and L

                                Triggers Vendor Management (M) review on vendor incidents

New Product Launch (L) ──▶ Pulls from Compliance (F), BSA (K), and Vendor Mgmt (M)
                            Output feeds operational workflows A–E and reporting in F

Core System Conversion (Q) ──▶ Stage-gated by F (compliance), K (BSA continuity),
                                J (security), M (vendor), and CFO/Audit sign-off

                         Competitive Intelligence Analyst feeds market scan into G and L

## 4. Workflow A — Member Onboarding

> **Objective**: Enroll new members with complete documentation, BSA/CIP compliance, and a product relationship that serves their actual financial needs — all completed same-day or next-day.

### 4.1 Active Agents

| Agent | Role | Primary Output |
|-------|------|----------------|
| **Member Services Representative** | Onboarding lead | Completed membership application and account setup |
| **BSA Officer** | CIP / identity verification | CIP documentation and SAR/CTR readiness |
| **Compliance Officer** | Regulatory sign-off | Compliance attestation for new member file |
| **Financial Advisor** | Needs discovery | Financial profile and referral recommendations |
| **Marketing Manager** | Welcome sequence | Onboarding communication and cross-sell activation |

### 4.2 Onboarding Sequence

```
STEP 1: Initial Contact and Eligibility (Member Services Rep)
├── Verify field of membership eligibility
├── Explain the cooperative difference: membership, ownership, dividends
├── Identify member's primary financial goal for joining
└── Collect government-issued ID and required documentation

STEP 2: CIP / BSA Verification (BSA Officer)
├── Verify identity per BSA Customer Identification Program
├── Screen against OFAC / SDN list
├── Screen against FinCEN 314(a) list
├── Beneficial ownership collection (if applicable — business accounts)
└── Document results in CIP file; flag any anomalies for SAR evaluation

STEP 3: Account Opening (Member Services Rep)
├── Open share savings account (establishes membership)
├── Collect signature card and account agreements
├── Deliver Truth in Savings disclosures (NCUA Part 707)
├── Deliver initial privacy notice (GLB)
├── Configure online banking / mobile app access
└── Set up eStatements preference

STEP 4: Needs Discovery (Financial Advisor — triggered on eligibility)
├── 5-minute financial wellness conversation: current banking, debts, goals
├── Identify immediate needs: checking account, auto loan, direct deposit
├── Identify future needs: mortgage, retirement, savings goals
└── Create referral list with priority and timeline

STEP 5: Cross-Sell Activation (Member Services Rep + Marketing Manager)
├── Open additional products per needs discovery: checking, debit card, auto-transfer
├── Enroll in appropriate marketing segments for follow-up campaigns
└── Trigger 30-day welcome email sequence

STEP 6: Quality Verification (Compliance Officer)
├── CIP file complete and identity verified
├── Required disclosures delivered and acknowledged
├── OFAC clear documented
└── New member file archived per records retention policy
```

### 4.3 Onboarding Quality Gate

**Gate Keeper**: Compliance Officer

| Criterion | Threshold | Evidence Required |
|-----------|-----------|-------------------|
| Field of membership eligibility verified | 100% of new members | Eligibility documentation in file |
| CIP identity verification complete | 100% — no exceptions | BSA Officer CIP file |
| OFAC/SDN screen negative (or documented resolution) | 100% | OFAC screen result in file |
| Required disclosures delivered | 100% | Signed/e-signed acknowledgment |
| Privacy notice delivered | 100% | Delivery log |

**Output**: Active member with complete compliant file → eligible for all products and services

## 5. Workflow B — Loan Origination

> **Objective**: Originate quality consumer loans through a structured credit analysis process that serves member financial wellbeing while maintaining sound asset quality — with full regulatory compliance at every step.

### 5.1 Active Agents

| Agent | Role | Primary Output |
|-------|------|----------------|
| **Loan Officer** | Origination lead | Loan analysis worksheet and credit decision |
| **Member Services Representative** | Application intake | Complete application package |
| **Compliance Officer** | ECOA / TILA compliance gate | Compliance verification before funding |
| **Risk Manager** | Portfolio risk assessment | Risk rating and exception documentation |
| **Financial Advisor** | Financial wellness screening | Debt consolidation opportunity or referral |
| **Collections Specialist** | Pre-funding delinquency check | Current account standing confirmation |

### 5.2 Loan Origination Sequence

```
STEP 1: Application and Pre-Qualification (Loan Officer)
├── Conduct member interview: purpose, amount, timeline, collateral
├── Pre-qualify verbally using current rate/product matrix
├── Obtain signed credit authorization
├── Pull tri-merge credit report; immediate fraud screen
└── Request income documentation per product matrix

STEP 2: Underwriting Analysis (Loan Officer)
├── Complete loan analysis worksheet (LTV, DTI, payment-to-income)
├── Identify policy exceptions; document compensating factors
├── Apply risk-based pricing matrix; assign credit grade
├── Prepare recommendation: Approve / Counter / Decline
└── Obtain approval authority sign-off per delegated limits

STEP 3: Compliance Review (Compliance Officer)
├── Verify ECOA compliance: no prohibited basis considered
├── Confirm application date captured for adverse action clock
├── Verify all required TILA disclosures prepared
└── Flag any HMDA reportable application for LAR recording

STEP 4: Decision Communication (Loan Officer)
├── Approve: call member, confirm terms, schedule closing
├── Counter-offer: call member, explain alternative structure
├── Decline: call member with primary reasons; issue Adverse Action Notice ≤30 days
└── Document call attempt and outcome in member notes

STEP 5: Closing and Funding (Loan Officer)
├── Verify all conditions satisfied
├── Prepare loan documents: Note, TILA disclosure, security agreement
├── Obtain member signatures (wet or e-sign)
├── Disburse funds; perfect collateral interest (file lien, hold title)
└── Board loan to core system: rate, term, payment, collateral coding

STEP 6: Post-Close Quality Check (Compliance Officer)
├── Verify Adverse Action Notice issued (if applicable) within 30 days
├── Verify TILA disclosure accuracy
├── Confirm HMDA data recorded (if mortgage-related inquiry)
└── Archive loan file per records retention schedule
```

### 5.3 Loan Origination Quality Gate

**Gate Keeper**: Loan Officer + Compliance Officer (dual sign-off on funded loans)

| Criterion | Threshold | Evidence Required |
|-----------|-----------|-------------------|
| Credit authorization signed before pull | 100% | Signed authorization in file |
| Loan analysis worksheet complete | 100% of approvals | Completed worksheet with DTI/LTV |
| Policy exceptions documented | 100% of exceptions | Exception form with compensating factors |
| TILA disclosure accurate and delivered | 100% | Member-signed TILA/Truth-in-Lending |
| Adverse action notice issued (for declines) | 100%, ≤30 days | Notice copy with delivery date |
| Lien perfected (secured loans) | 100% | Title/lien confirmation |

**Output**: Boarded loan with complete compliant file → eligible for servicing and Collections monitoring

## 6. Workflow C — Mortgage Processing

> **Objective**: Process mortgage applications from application to closing with full TRID compliance, secondary market readiness, and a closing that the member understands and can sign with confidence.

### 6.1 Active Agents

| Agent | Role | Primary Output |
|-------|------|----------------|
| **Mortgage Loan Processor** | Processing lead and TRID compliance owner | Complete, saleable loan file |
| **Loan Officer** | Origination and member relationship | Application, rate lock, decision |
| **Compliance Officer** | Regulatory compliance gate | TRID, HMDA, and RESPA compliance attestation |
| **Risk Manager** | Underwriting exception review | Exception approval and risk documentation |
| **CFO** | Pipeline and interest rate risk | Rate lock and hedging decisions |
| **Internal Auditor** | Pre-close file audit (for large loans) | Pre-closing quality review |

### 6.2 Mortgage Processing Sequence

```
STAGE 1: Application (Days 1-3)
├── Loan Officer: Application interview; pre-qualification
├── Loan Officer: Deliver Loan Estimate within 3 business days of application
├── Mortgage Loan Processor: Receive file; open processing checklist
├── Compliance Officer: Confirm Loan Estimate timing and accuracy
└── CFO: Rate lock decision per pipeline policy

STAGE 2: Processing (Days 4-20)
├── Mortgage Loan Processor: Order appraisal, title search, flood cert
├── Mortgage Loan Processor: Collect and verify all income/asset documents
├── Mortgage Loan Processor: Run automated underwriting (DU/LP/NCUA guidelines)
├── Mortgage Loan Processor: Order any required inspections
├── Risk Manager: Review if exceptions to guidelines required
└── Compliance Officer: HMDA data collection verification

STAGE 3: Underwriting (Days 15-25)
├── Loan Officer: Final credit decision (approve/suspend/deny)
├── Mortgage Loan Processor: Satisfy all underwriting conditions
├── Compliance Officer: Verify change-of-circumstance disclosures if applicable
└── Risk Manager: Document exception approvals with compensating factors

STAGE 4: Closing Preparation (Days 25-40)
├── Mortgage Loan Processor: Prepare Closing Disclosure; deliver 3 business days before closing
├── Compliance Officer: Verify CD accuracy and timing compliance
├── Mortgage Loan Processor: Coordinate with title company and seller
├── Internal Auditor: Pre-close file review (loans above threshold)
└── Loan Officer: Final conditions satisfied sign-off

STAGE 5: Closing and Funding (Days 40-45)
├── Loan Officer: Closing table — explain all documents to member
├── Mortgage Loan Processor: Fund after right-of-rescission period (refi only)
├── Mortgage Loan Processor: Perfect lien (record deed of trust)
├── Compliance Officer: Final HMDA disposition recorded
└── CFO: Loan boarded; pipeline hedge closed

STAGE 6: Post-Closing
├── Mortgage Loan Processor: Ship file to secondary market (if applicable)
├── Mortgage Loan Processor: Final file compliance check
└── Compliance Officer: Annual HMDA LAR certification
```

### 6.3 Mortgage Quality Gate

**Gate Keeper**: Mortgage Loan Processor + Compliance Officer

| Criterion | Threshold | Evidence Required |
|-----------|-----------|-------------------|
| Loan Estimate delivered ≤3 business days of application | 100% | LE delivery timestamp |
| Closing Disclosure delivered ≥3 business days before close | 100% | CD delivery timestamp |
| Appraisal ordered from approved appraiser | 100% | Appraisal order confirmation |
| All income/assets independently verified | 100% | Verification documents in file |
| TRID change-of-circumstance disclosures issued when required | 100% | COC documentation |
| Right-of-rescission period honored (refinances) | 100% | Wait period documentation |
| HMDA data recorded | 100% of HMDA-reportable loans | LAR data entry |

**Output**: Closed loan with investor-ready file and complete regulatory documentation

## 7. Workflow D — Collections & Recovery

> **Objective**: Resolve delinquency at the earliest possible stage through member-centered outreach that protects both the member's financial health and the credit union's asset quality.

### 7.1 Active Agents

| Agent | Role | Primary Output |
|-------|------|----------------|
| **Collections Specialist** | Delinquency management lead | Resolved delinquency or recovery plan |
| **Loan Officer** | Loan modification / restructure | Workout agreement |
| **Financial Advisor** | Financial wellness intervention | Budget assessment and referral plan |
| **Compliance Officer** | FDCPA / FCRA compliance gate | Compliance monitoring on collections practices |
| **Risk Manager** | Charge-off and loss reserve | Loss mitigation recommendation and reserve adjustment |
| **Member Services Representative** | Account access and history | Complete member relationship picture |

### 7.2 Collections Workflow by Stage

```
STAGE 1: Early Delinquency (1-29 days past due)
├── Collections Specialist: Automated payment reminder (Day 5)
├── Collections Specialist: First live outreach attempt (Day 10)
├── Collections Specialist: Second attempt — alternate contact methods (Day 20)
├── Financial Advisor: Triggered if member indicates financial hardship
└── Goal: Cure before 30-day reporting to credit bureaus

STAGE 2: 30-59 Days Past Due
├── Collections Specialist: Intensified outreach — phone + written notice
├── Collections Specialist: Request payment arrangement or hardship review
├── Loan Officer: Evaluate loan modification or extension options
├── Compliance Officer: Verify FDCPA-compliant communication practices
└── Goal: Payment arrangement in place or formal workout agreement

STAGE 3: 60-89 Days Past Due
├── Collections Specialist: Escalated collection effort
├── Loan Officer: Formal workout proposal — deferral, extension, rate reduction
├── Risk Manager: Loan review for specific reserve; loss probability assessment
├── Compliance Officer: Review all communication for FDCPA compliance
└── Goal: Workout agreement executed or repossession/legal action initiated

STAGE 4: 90+ Days Past Due — Loss Mitigation
├── Risk Manager: Charge-off recommendation if loss is probable
├── Collections Specialist: Demand letter; acceleration of secured loans
├── Loan Officer: Final workout opportunity before legal action
├── Collections Specialist: Coordinate repossession (auto) or initiate foreclosure (real estate)
└── Goal: Maximize recovery; protect credit union from loss; preserve member dignity

STAGE 5: Post-Charge-Off Recovery
├── Collections Specialist: Continue recovery effort post-charge-off
├── Risk Manager: Report charge-offs to NCUA Call Report; update ALLL
├── Collections Specialist: Negotiate settlements; manage collector/attorney placements
└── Goal: Maximize recovery percentage on charged-off portfolio
```

### 7.3 Collections Quality Gate

**Gate Keeper**: Collections Specialist + Compliance Officer

| Criterion | Threshold | Evidence Required |
|-----------|-----------|-------------------|
| FDCPA-compliant communications | 100% | Communication log with timestamps |
| Credit bureau reporting accurate | 100% — monthly | Verified data furnishing records |
| Workout agreements documented | 100% | Signed modification agreement |
| Charge-off recommendation reviewed by Risk Manager | 100% | Risk Manager sign-off |
| NCUA delinquency and charge-off reporting accurate | 100% | Call Report reconciliation |

**Output**: Resolved delinquency (cured, workout, or charged-off with documentation)

## 8. Workflow E — Deposit Operations

> **Objective**: Maintain accurate, compliant deposit accounts — opens, maintenance, rate changes, exceptions, and closures — while supporting BSA/AML monitoring and funding the credit union's balance sheet.

### 8.1 Active Agents

| Agent | Role | Primary Output |
|-------|------|----------------|
| **Deposit Operations Manager** | Operations lead | Accurate, compliant deposit accounts |
| **BSA Officer** | Transaction monitoring | SAR filings, CTR processing, AML alerts |
| **CFO** | Rate setting and liquidity | Deposit rate decisions aligned to ALCO |
| **Compliance Officer** | Reg CC, Reg E, Truth in Savings | Compliance verification |

## 20. Workflow Q — Core System Conversion

> **Objective**: Execute a core banking system migration that protects member data, maintains regulatory compliance throughout the transition, and delivers a reliable go-live with zero data loss and zero compliance gaps. Core conversions are the highest-risk IT events a credit union undertakes — every phase is gated, every risk is documented, and no go-live proceeds without evidence of readiness.

### 20.1 Active Agents

| Agent | Role | Primary Output |
|-------|------|----------------|
| **CTO** | Conversion sponsor and architect | Vendor selection; conversion plan |
| **Core Systems Administrator** | Conversion lead and gate keeper | Validated data conversion; live system |
| **CIO** | Information security and data governance | Security and data integrity sign-off |
| **CFO** | Budget, GL mapping, financial reporting validation | GL parity post-cutover |
| **Compliance Officer** | Regulatory reporting continuity (HMDA, Call Report) | Compliance reporting validated |
| **Internal Auditor** | Independent test and stage-gate review | Independent sign-off at each gate |
| **BSA Officer** | BSA monitoring continuity | BSA data and rules validated |
| **CEO** | Project sponsor and external comms | Board, member, and regulator communications |

### 20.2 Sequence (Stage-Gated)

```
PHASE 1: Vendor Selection (CTO + CFO)
├── RFP to qualified vendors; SOC 2 review; reference checks
├── TCO and contract review (legal counsel)
└── Board approval of selected vendor

PHASE 2: Requirements & Project Planning (CTO + CIO + Core Systems Admin)
├── Detailed scope; data mapping; integration inventory
├── Project plan with stage gates and risk register
└── Notify members per NCUA material service change rules

PHASE 3: Build & Configuration (Core Systems Admin + Software Engineer)
├── Configure new core; implement integrations
├── BSA monitoring rules ported; OFAC screening intact
└── HMDA, Call Report, and GL mapping validated

PHASE 4: Test (Core Systems Admin + Internal Auditor + Compliance Officer)
├── Functional testing; data conversion validation; reconciliation
├── Parallel run; performance and DR testing
└── BSA Officer, CFO, Compliance, Audit independent sign-offs

PHASE 5: Cutover (CTO + Core Systems Admin)
├── Go/no-go gate review with stage-gate handoff (template #16)
├── Cutover weekend; member-facing communication active
├── Fallback plan validated and ready
└── Day-1 stabilization with elevated monitoring

PHASE 6: Stabilization (90 days)
├── Defect triage; performance tuning
├── Member experience monitoring; concierge support
└── Internal Auditor post-conversion review

PHASE 7: Closeout
├── Legacy system data archive per BSA retention
├── Lessons learned to board
└── Final regulator notification of completion
```

### 20.3 Quality Gate

**Gate Keeper**: Core Systems Administrator + Compliance Officer

| Criterion | Threshold | Evidence |
|-----------|-----------|----------|

| Data conversion variance within tolerance | ≤ policy threshold | Reconciliation report |
| Parallel run completed and reconciled | Required | Parallel run report |
| Fallback plan validated | Required | Fallback test record |
| Member notice per NCUA material service change | 100% | Notice records |
| BSA, HMDA, GL continuity validated | 100% | Validation reports |

**Output**: Live new core with validated data, continuous compliance, and documented lessons learned.
