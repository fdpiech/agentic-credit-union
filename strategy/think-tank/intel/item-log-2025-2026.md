## Q1–Q2 2025 — CFPB Upheaval

*The consumer protection landscape shifts dramatically. The CFPB is curtailed, major rules are reversed or frozen, but the underlying statutes remain. CANVAS must decide what still applies and what has changed.*

### INTEL-ALL-008 — CFPB Operational Curtailment

**Identified**: 2025-02 | **Reviewer**: Compliance Officer | **Review date**: 2026-04-12
**Workflow(s)**: B, C, D, E, H, I, P (all consumer-facing)

**Assessment**: This is the single largest shift in the federal consumer compliance landscape in a decade. CFPB supervision events cut 50%, 70 interpretive rules rescinded, focus shifted to depositories. However, the underlying statutes (TILA, ECOA, FCRA, UDAAP, Reg E) are unchanged — Congress didn't repeal them. State AGs may fill the enforcement gap, creating fragmented compliance expectations. CANVAS workflows that reference CFPB guidance need to distinguish between "guidance withdrawn" and "statutory obligation removed" — they are not the same thing.

**Recommendation**: defer-to-cycle
**Rationale**: The compliance gates in Workflows B, C, D, and H reference specific regulatory obligations. Whether those gates need to change depends on which obligations were guidance-based (now withdrawn) vs. statute-based (still binding). This requires a systematic review under stress — not a quick edit.
**Target cycle**: Next cycle on Workflow B (Lending cluster) or Workflow F (Compliance Examination)

**Disposition**: _Pending approver review_

### INTEL-ALL-010 — Overdraft Rule Repealed via CRA

**Identified**: 2025-04 | **Reviewer**: Compliance Officer | **Review date**: 2026-04-12
**Workflow(s)**: E (Deposit Ops), I (Card Services)

**Assessment**: The overdraft fee cap is dead, and the CRA prohibition means the CFPB can't issue a "substantially similar" rule. But market pressure continues — consumers saved $6B/year from voluntary fee reductions, and 80% of large CUs still charge NSF fees (vs. 35% of banks). The Navy Federal $95M action shows egregious practices still face enforcement. For CANVAS: the fee environment is permissive but the competitive/reputational trend is toward lower fees. This is more of a strategic positioning question than a compliance gate question.

**Recommendation**: monitoring
**Rationale**: No immediate framework change needed — the rule was repealed before it took effect. But the market trend toward fee reduction is real and may affect how Workflow E models fee decisions. Monitor for whether competitive pressure or state-level action forces fee changes.
**Review trigger**: State AG enforcement on overdraft/NSF fees, or a peer CU of similar size eliminates fees and gains measurable member growth

**Disposition**: _Pending approver review_

### INTEL-ALL-009 — Section 1033 Open Banking Rule Frozen

**Identified**: 2025-05 | **Reviewer**: Compliance Officer | **Review date**: 2026-04-12
**Workflow(s)**: E (Deposit Ops), A (Member Onboarding), J (IT Security), P (Financial Wellness)

**Assessment**: The CFPB itself asked the court to vacate Section 1033, calling it beyond statutory authority. A comprehensive reexamination is underway with no clear timeline. CUs under $850M were exempt regardless. For CANVAS: the data-sharing infrastructure, third-party access controls, and member consent workflows that 1033 would have required are on indefinite hold. However, fintech aggregators still screen-scrape, and member expectations for data portability are set by market reality, not regulation. The rule may return in different form.

**Recommendation**: monitoring
**Rationale**: No compliance obligation exists. But the underlying market force (members expect data portability) hasn't stopped. If a revised rule emerges or if screen-scraping incidents create liability exposure, CANVAS will need data-sharing workflows.
**Review trigger**: CFPB publishes revised proposed rule, or a CU faces litigation over screen-scraping-related data breach

**Disposition**: _Pending approver review_

### INTEL-ALL-012 — CFPB Supervision Refocused on Depository Fraud

**Identified**: 2025-Q2 | **Reviewer**: Compliance Officer | **Review date**: 2026-04-12
**Workflow(s)**: H (Fraud/Dispute), B (Loan Origination), C (Mortgage)

**Assessment**: Even with reduced CFPB activity, what remains is explicitly aimed at depository institutions and "actual fraud against consumers with identifiable victims and measurable damages." Mortgages are highest priority. Veterans and service members are a focus population. This means Workflows B, C, and H are still under active federal supervision — credit unions should not assume CFPB curtailment means zero oversight.

**Recommendation**: incorporate
**Rationale**: Workflow C (Mortgage) and Workflow H (Fraud/Dispute) should note that CFPB supervision continues to target these areas even during the broader curtailment. A brief note in the compliance context section of each playbook ensures operators don't assume a fully permissive environment.
**Target file**: `strategy/playbooks/workflow-c-mortgage-processing.md`, `strategy/playbooks/workflow-h-fraud-dispute.md`

**Disposition**: _Pending approver review_

**Identified**: 2025 | **Reviewer**: Compliance Officer | **Review date**: 2026-04-12
**Workflow(s)**: B (Loan Origination)

**Assessment**: Compliance dates extended to 2026-2027 (tier-dependent), but the rule is not repealed — it's delayed. Credit unions with business lending programs will need demographic data collection infrastructure by their tier deadline. CANVAS Workflow B currently does not differentiate consumer vs. business lending data collection requirements. This is a known gap that will become actionable when compliance dates approach.

**Recommendation**: monitoring
**Rationale**: The compliance deadline hasn't arrived for most CUs, but the rule exists and is final. When the Tier 1 date (July 2026) approaches, Workflow B will need a business-lending data collection step or branch.
**Review trigger**: 90 days before Tier 1 compliance date (April 2026) — or if the rule is further delayed/repealed
