## How to Read This Log

Items are grouped by the quarter they were identified, matching the chronological order a real CU would process them.

- **defer-to-cycle** — real finding, needs stress testing first
- **monitoring** — signal is real but not yet actionable; set a review trigger

## Q1–Q2 2025 — CFPB Upheaval

*The consumer protection landscape shifts dramatically. The CFPB is curtailed, major rules are reversed or frozen, but the underlying statutes remain. CANVAS must decide what still applies and what has changed.*

### INTEL-ALL-008 — CFPB Operational Curtailment

**Identified**: 2025-02 | **Reviewer**: Compliance Officer | **Review date**: 2026-04-12
**Workflow(s)**: B, C, D, E, H, I, P (all consumer-facing)

**Assessment**: This is the single largest shift in the federal consumer compliance landscape in a decade. CFPB supervision events cut 50%, 70 interpretive rules rescinded, focus shifted to depositories. However, the underlying statutes (TILA, ECOA, FCRA, UDAAP, Reg E) are unchanged — Congress didn't repeal them. State AGs may fill the enforcement gap, creating fragmented compliance expectations. CANVAS workflows that reference CFPB guidance need to distinguish between "guidance withdrawn" and "statutory obligation removed" — they are not the same thing.

**Recommendation**: defer-to-cycle
**Rationale**: The compliance gates in Workflows B, C, D, and H reference specific regulatory obligations. Whether those gates need to change depends on which obligations were guidance-based (now withdrawn) vs. statute-based (still binding). This requires a systematic review under stress — not a quick edit.
**Target cycle**: Next cycle on Workflow B (Lending cluster) or Workflow F (Compliance Examination)

### INTEL-ALL-007 — NCUA Removes Disparate Impact from Fair Lending

**Identified**: 2025-09 | **Reviewer**: Compliance Officer | **Review date**: 2026-04-12
**Workflow(s)**: B (Loan Origination), C (Mortgage), F (Compliance Examination)

**Assessment**: NCUA examiners will no longer review for disparate impact — only for disparate treatment (intentional discrimination). HMDA data analysis continues. This narrows the fair lending examination scope significantly: CUs no longer need to maintain disparate impact risk assessments or analyze policies for unintentional discriminatory effects under NCUA examination. However, the Equal Credit Opportunity Act and Fair Housing Act still exist as statutes. Private litigation under disparate impact theories may continue even if NCUA doesn't examine for it. For CANVAS: lending workflow compliance gates that reference disparate impact analysis should be updated to reflect the current exam scope while noting the continuing statutory risk.

**Recommendation**: incorporate
**Rationale**: Workflow B and C compliance gates should reflect the narrowed exam scope (disparate treatment only). Workflow F exam preparation should remove disparate impact risk assessment from NCUA-specific preparation steps. The change is concrete and bounded. A note should be preserved that the statutory basis for disparate impact claims has not been repealed — only the NCUA examination focus has changed.
**Target file**: `strategy/playbooks/workflow-b-loan-origination.md`, `strategy/playbooks/workflow-c-mortgage-processing.md`, `strategy/playbooks/workflow-f-compliance-examination.md`
