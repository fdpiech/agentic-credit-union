# Workflow N Playbook — Branch Operations & Expansion

> **Cycle Time**: 3–12 months (new branch) – Ongoing (existing operations) | **Agents**: 6 | **Gate Keepers**: Branch Manager + Compliance Officer

---

## Objective

Open new branch locations and maintain ongoing branch operations in full compliance with NCUA field of membership rules, state branch notification requirements, and ADA accessibility standards — while ensuring each location is financially viable, adequately staffed, and integrated with core systems before accepting the first member transaction.

## Pre-Conditions

- [ ] Board resolution authorizing new branch feasibility study (new branch) or annual operations review initiated (existing branch)
- [ ] CEO strategic plan includes branch footprint expansion or optimization
- [ ] CFO confirms capital adequacy to support branch investment without impairing net worth ratio
- [ ] Proposed location falls within current field of membership or FOM expansion is planned concurrently

## Regulatory Framework

| Regulation | Requirement | Enforcement Agent |
|------------|-------------|-------------------|
| NCUA Part 701.36 | Prior written approval required for new branch office; submit to regional office ≥ 90 days before opening | Compliance Officer |
| NCUA FOM Rules (Part 701, Appendix B) | All members served by the branch must qualify under the credit union's current charter | Compliance Officer + CEO |
| ADA Title III (28 CFR Part 36) | Physical location must be fully accessible; parking, entrance, teller counters, ATMs, and signage compliant | Branch Manager + CTO |
| State Branch Notification Laws | Most states require separate state regulator notification or approval concurrent with NCUA filing | Compliance Officer |
| NCUA Safety & Soundness (Part 741) | Branch must not impair net worth ratio; CAMEL impact analysis required in application | CFO + CEO |
| Reg E / Reg CC | All ATMs and transaction equipment must meet current EFT and funds availability requirements | CTO + Compliance Officer |

## Agent Activation Sequence

### Step 1: Site Selection & Business Case (CEO + CFO + Branch Manager)

```
Activate CEO, CFO, and Branch Manager for branch site selection and business case development.

Project: Branch [Name / Working Title] | Target market area: [City, Region, or ZIP cluster]
Trigger: Board authorization received [Date] | Feasibility study deadline: [Date]

CEO deliverables:
1. Define strategic rationale: membership growth, geographic gap, employer group concentration, or defensive competitive response
2. Identify 2–3 candidate sites; document proximity to existing members, Select Employee Groups (SEGs), and competitor branch density
3. Confirm proposed branch type: full-service, limited-service, drive-through only, or ITM/video-teller kiosk
4. Present recommendation to board with strategic narrative

CFO deliverables:
1. Build 3-year pro forma for each candidate site:
   - Build-out / lease cost
   - Staffing cost (FTE model)
   - Projected new member and deposit growth (conservative / base / optimistic)
   - Break-even timeline
2. Assess impact on net worth ratio and liquidity position
3. Confirm capital allocation is within policy limits without board capital plan amendment
4. Recommend site based on risk-adjusted ROI

Branch Manager deliverables:
1. Complete market analysis: household density, income demographics, traffic counts
2. Assess lease or purchase terms; involve legal counsel for LOI review
3. Identify community anchors: employers, schools, civic organizations within 1-mile radius
4. Document staffing model: FTEs needed at opening vs. steady state (18 months)

Output: Branch Business Case document approved by CEO and CFO before Step 2 begins
Timeline: 30–60 days from board authorization
```

### Step 2: Field of Membership Analysis (Compliance Officer + CEO)

```
Activate Compliance Officer and CEO for field of membership eligibility analysis.

Branch site: [Address] | Charter type: [Community / SEG-based / Association-based]
Current FOM boundary: [Description or charter document reference]

Compliance Officer deliverables:
1. Map proposed branch service area against current charter FOM definition
2. Identify whether all anticipated member segments at new location are within existing FOM:
   - Community charter: confirm the proposed area is within the approved community
   - SEG-based: confirm SEGs in target area are on the approved list or require addition
   - Association-based: confirm eligible association membership in target area
3. If FOM expansion is required, prepare NCUA FOM amendment application (concurrent with branch application):
   - Community charter expansion: NCUA Regional Director approval required
   - SEG addition: employer documentation, congressional district verification, HR contact
4. Document analysis in writing; retain for NCUA application file
5. Coordinate with state regulator if state FOM rules differ from federal

CEO deliverables:
1. Sign off on FOM analysis; confirm no members will be served outside charter FOM
2. If FOM expansion required: present to board and obtain board resolution before filing with NCUA
3. Engage NCUA Regional Director in pre-application call to align on timeline

Compliance flags:
- DO NOT open branch to any member segment not within current or approved FOM
- FOM expansion and branch application may be submitted simultaneously but must both be approved before opening
- Document basis for FOM eligibility of the target market in the NCUA application

Timeline: 20–30 days concurrent with Step 1 business case
```

### Step 3: Regulatory Approval (Compliance Officer + CEO)

```
Activate Compliance Officer and CEO for NCUA branch approval application.

Pre-conditions:
- [ ] Business case approved by board
- [ ] FOM analysis complete — all served members within current or concurrently amended FOM
- [ ] Site selected and LOI or lease term sheet executed

NCUA Branch Application (Part 701.36) deliverables:
1. Complete NCUA prior approval application; submit to Regional Office ≥ 90 days before planned opening:
   - Business plan with 3-year financial projections
   - FOM analysis confirming all members in branch service area qualify
   - Proof of site control (executed lease or purchase agreement)
   - Board resolution authorizing the application
   - Net worth ratio analysis — confirm branch will not cause ratio to fall below 7%
   - Capital adequacy narrative
2. Submit concurrent state regulator notification or application per state law
3. ADA pre-compliance certification: architect/contractor confirms design meets ADA Title III
4. Maintain NCUA application tracking log; respond to any NCUA requests within 10 business days

Post-approval:
1. Record NCUA approval letter; archive in board minute book
2. Confirm any conditions attached to approval are tracked for satisfaction before opening
3. Notify state regulator of NCUA approval (where required)

Compliance flags:
- NEVER accept member transactions or deposits before NCUA approval is received in writing
- If NCUA requests additional information, track the clock carefully — delays beyond 90 days require updated financial projections
- Approval conditions (e.g., staffing minimums, opening deposit thresholds) must be satisfied and documented before Day 1

Timeline: 90–120 days (NCUA processing); plan for 6–9 months total from application to opening
```

### Step 4: Staffing Model & Hiring (CHRO + Branch Manager)

```
Activate CHRO and Branch Manager for branch staffing model development and hiring.

Branch: [Name] | Planned opening: [Date] | NCUA approval received: [Date]
Staffing model approved by CFO: [FTE count and job classification summary]

CHRO deliverables:
1. Develop staffing plan with job classifications, pay grades, and benefits alignment:
   - Branch Manager (if not existing internal transfer)
   - Member Services Representatives (teller/MSR hybrid)
   - Loan Officer (if full-service branch)
   - Part-time / flex staff for peak periods
2. Post positions; prioritize internal mobility candidates before external postings
3. Conduct EEOC-compliant hiring process; document interview criteria and scoring
4. Complete I-9 and background check process for all new hires
5. Coordinate benefits enrollment for all new staff effective on opening date

Branch Manager deliverables:
1. Participate in candidate interviews; provide final recommendation to CHRO
2. Develop training schedule: all staff complete product knowledge, member service standards, BSA/AML, and security training before opening
3. Assign mentors from existing branches for new staff shadowing (minimum 2 weeks pre-opening)
4. Create opening-week schedule including soft-open staff rehearsal day

Timeline: Hiring complete at least 6 weeks before planned opening date
Training: All certifications complete at least 2 weeks before opening
```

### Step 5: Equipment, Technology & Installation (CTO + IT Infrastructure Engineer)

```
Activate CTO and IT Infrastructure Engineer for branch technology buildout.

Branch: [Name] | Occupancy date: [Date] | Target opening: [Date]
Core system: [System name] | Network provider: [Name]

CTO deliverables:
1. Design branch technology stack:
   - Teller workstations connected to core system (live network, not VPN fallback only)
   - ATM installation: ADA-compliant (audio jack, Braille keypad, screen height); Reg E compliant fee disclosures
   - Digital signage and member-facing rate displays
   - Security camera system and vault access controls
   - ITM / video teller unit (if applicable) — test connectivity to Video Teller Center
2. Coordinate with core system vendor for new branch activation; test all transaction types before opening
3. Establish network redundancy: primary fiber + backup LTE failover
4. Complete PCI DSS compliance review for all card-processing equipment
5. Install and test online banking enrollment stations (member-facing tablets)

IT Infrastructure Engineer deliverables:
1. Install all physical equipment per architect's floor plan
2. Execute core system connectivity test with branch staff (run 50 simulated transactions in test environment)
3. Document all equipment serial numbers, warranty dates, and vendor contacts
4. Establish IT helpdesk escalation path specific to new branch
5. Test alarm, access control, and surveillance integration

ADA technology checklist:
- [ ] ATM audio access operational (tested with headphones)
- [ ] Screen height adjustable or fixed at ADA-compliant level
- [ ] Teller counter accessible height section installed
- [ ] Member lobby accessible path confirmed clear

Timeline: Equipment installation complete 3 weeks before opening; technology testing complete 1 week before opening
```

### Step 6: Pre-Opening Member Communication (Marketing Manager + Branch Manager)

```
Activate Marketing Manager and Branch Manager for pre-opening community outreach.

Branch: [Name] | Opening date: [Date]
Target service area: [Description] | Key employer groups in area: [List]

Marketing Manager deliverables:
1. Develop grand opening campaign (launch 6 weeks before opening date):
   - Geo-targeted digital advertising to households within 5-mile radius
   - Email campaign to existing members with addresses in the branch service area
   - Local employer outreach: offer on-site financial wellness presentation to SEG HR contacts
   - Community sponsorship: identify local event or nonprofit to sponsor concurrent with opening
2. Create opening promotions (board-approved rate specials or product bundles) — confirm UDAAP compliance with Compliance Officer before advertising
3. Prepare branch-specific promotional materials, signage, and collateral
4. Submit all advertising for Compliance Officer review before publication — no exceptions

Branch Manager deliverables:
1. Conduct employer group outreach: personal visits to top 5 employers in service area
2. Coordinate soft-open event for VIP members, community leaders, and board members (1 week before grand opening)
3. Brief all staff on opening promotions, talking points, and local community context
4. Confirm all third-party vendors (armored car, coin machine servicer, cleaning) have opening-day service agreements

Compliance flag:
- All advertising must disclose membership eligibility requirements per NCUA charter
- Rate specials must include APR and term disclosures per TILA/Reg Z and Truth in Savings (Reg DD)

Timeline: Campaign live 6 weeks before opening; all materials Compliance Officer-approved before launch
```

### Step 7: Grand Opening & Post-Opening Review (Branch Manager + CEO)

```
Activate Branch Manager and CEO for grand opening execution and 90-day post-opening review.

Branch: [Name] | Opening date: [Date] | NCUA approval: CONFIRMED
All pre-conditions satisfied: [ ] Technology tested | [ ] Staff trained | [ ] NCUA approval in hand

Opening Day:
1. Branch Manager conducts pre-opening staff briefing (60 minutes before door open)
2. Confirm vault cash and coin supply adequate for projected opening-day volume
3. Confirm all teller drawers balanced to zero before first transaction
4. CEO or designee present at opening; greet first members personally
5. Log first 100 accounts opened for tracking against business case projections

90-Day Post-Opening Review (CEO + CFO + Branch Manager):
1. Compare actual membership growth, deposit growth, and loan production to business case projections
2. Review staffing adequacy: are FTE hours matching member volume by time-of-day?
3. Confirm ADA compliance inspection completed (within 60 days of opening)
4. Confirm all NCUA approval conditions satisfied; file confirmation with NCUA if required
5. Identify any operational issues from staff and member feedback; create resolution log
6. Present 90-day results to board with go-forward projections and any corrective actions

Timeline: 90-day review presented to board at next regularly scheduled meeting after Day 90
```

## Quality Gate Checklist

| # | Criterion | Threshold | Evidence Required | Status |
|---|-----------|-----------|-------------------|--------|
| 1 | NCUA prior approval received in writing before any member transactions | 100% — no exceptions | NCUA approval letter on file | ☐ |
| 2 | All members served fall within current FOM | 100% | FOM analysis document signed by Compliance Officer | ☐ |
| 3 | ADA compliance confirmed by architect or inspector | 100% | Signed ADA compliance certification | ☐ |
| 4 | State regulator notification/approval completed | 100% | State approval or acknowledgment on file | ☐ |
| 5 | All staff completed required training before opening | 100% | Training completion records for all employees | ☐ |
| 6 | Core system connectivity tested with 50+ simulated transactions | 100% | IT test log signed by CTO | ☐ |
| 7 | All marketing materials approved by Compliance Officer before publication | 100% | Compliance Officer sign-off on each piece | ☐ |
| 8 | 90-day post-opening review presented to board | 100% | Board minutes reflecting review | ☐ |

**Gate Keepers**: Branch Manager (operational readiness) + Compliance Officer (regulatory approval and FOM compliance) — dual sign-off required before Day 1 transactions

**Gate Decision**:
- **OPEN**: All criteria met — branch is cleared to open and accept member transactions
- **DELAY**: Specific open items identified — Branch Manager resolves within 5 business days; Compliance Officer re-evaluates
- **BLOCKED**: NCUA approval not received, FOM deficiency identified, or ADA deficiency not corrected — branch CANNOT open; CEO notified immediately

## Branch Operations Performance Metrics

| Metric | Target | Measurement Frequency |
|--------|--------|-----------------------|
| New members opened at branch (first 90 days) | ≥ business case projection ± 15% | Monthly |
| Deposit balance growth | Break-even trajectory by month 18 | Monthly |
| Teller transaction error rate | < 0.5% of transactions | Monthly |
| Member wait time (teller line) | < 5 minutes average | Weekly (branch log) |
| ADA complaint rate | Zero unresolved complaints | Monthly |
| Staff turnover at branch (first 12 months) | < 20% annualized | Quarterly |
| 90-day business case variance (loans + deposits) | ≤ 20% below projection | 90-day review |

---

*Workflow N is complete when the Compliance Officer confirms NCUA approval conditions are satisfied, the Branch Manager confirms operational readiness across all systems and staff, and the 90-day post-opening review has been presented to the board with no unresolved compliance findings.*
