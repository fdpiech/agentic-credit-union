# Workflow O Playbook — Merger & Acquisition

> **Cycle Time**: 12–24 months | **Agents**: 8 | **Gate Keepers**: CEO + Compliance Officer

---

## Objective

Execute a credit union merger or acquisition that results in a financially stronger combined institution, uninterrupted member service, and full regulatory compliance — from initial identification through post-merger integration. Every merger must survive NCUA examination scrutiny, pass a member vote, and close with zero member data or service disruption.

## Pre-Conditions

- [ ] Board has adopted a written inorganic growth strategy or received an approach from a potential merger partner
- [ ] CEO has authority to execute a Letter of Intent or has obtained specific board authorization to proceed
- [ ] Credit union net worth ratio is at or above 7% (well-capitalized) and projected to remain so post-merger
- [ ] No unresolved NCUA or state regulator enforcement actions at either institution

## Regulatory Framework

| Regulation | Requirement | Enforcement Agent |
|------------|-------------|-------------------|
| NCUA Part 708b | Governs all federally insured credit union mergers; application required; NCUA approval mandatory | Compliance Officer + CEO |
| Member Vote Requirement (708b.8) | Merging credit union members must vote; 35-day advance notice to members required before vote | CEO + Marketing Manager |
| NCUA Letter of Intent | Notify NCUA Regional Director of intent to merge before submitting formal application | CEO + Compliance Officer |
| State Regulator Notification | State-chartered CUs require state regulator approval concurrent with NCUA process | Compliance Officer |
| Hart-Scott-Rodino (HSR) Act | Federal antitrust filing required if combined assets exceed HSR thresholds (~$119M); rare for CUs | CEO + external counsel |
| NCUA Share Insurance (Part 745) | Member share accounts at the merged CU remain insured; combined balances may exceed $250K per ownership category — disclose to affected members | Compliance Officer |
| BSA/AML (31 CFR 1020) | Acquirer inherits BSA obligations; full AML program review of target required during due diligence | BSA Officer |
| FCRA / Data Privacy | Member data from target CU must be handled under privacy and data security policies from closing date | CTO + Compliance Officer |

## Agent Activation Sequence

### Step 1: Target Identification & Initial Assessment (CEO + CFO)

```
Activate CEO and CFO for merger target identification and preliminary assessment.

Context: [Inbound approach from target / Proactive identification / Board directive]
Target institution: [Name] | Charter type: [Federal / State] | Asset size: ~$[Amount]
Geographic footprint: [Description] | Member count: ~[Number]

CEO deliverables:
1. Assess strategic fit:
   - Does target's FOM overlap or complement our current charter?
   - Does target bring desirable capabilities (mortgage portfolio, business lending, specific SEGs)?
   - What is the competitive landscape rationale?
2. Conduct high-level reputational review:
   - NCUA examination history (call report data, public enforcement actions)
   - News search for litigation, member complaints, or adverse press
   - Leadership stability (have they had 2+ CEOs in 3 years?)
3. Assess cultural alignment: cooperative values, member service philosophy, staff culture
4. Prepare preliminary board briefing — NO public disclosure; all discussions under NDA

CFO deliverables:
1. Pull target's 5990 call report data (last 8 quarters from NCUA public database)
2. Calculate preliminary financial health indicators:
   - Net worth ratio trend
   - Loan delinquency rate and charge-off history
   - Return on assets
   - Loan-to-share ratio
3. Estimate combined institution financial position: net worth, capital adequacy, liquidity
4. Flag any financial red flags requiring deep due diligence before proceeding

Output: Preliminary assessment memo to board (CONFIDENTIAL) with go/no-go recommendation
Timeline: 30–45 days from initial contact or board directive
```

### Step 2: Letter of Intent (CEO + Board)

```
Activate CEO for Letter of Intent preparation and board authorization.

Pre-condition: Board has reviewed preliminary assessment and voted to proceed
Target: [Name] | Target CEO: [Name] | Target board chair: [Name]

CEO deliverables:
1. Draft Letter of Intent (LOI) covering:
   - Proposed merger structure (continuation CU, merged CU, or new CU)
   - Surviving charter (which institution continues)
   - Proposed membership conversion: target members become our members on closing date
   - Exclusivity period (typically 60–90 days during due diligence)
   - Confidentiality obligations
   - Non-binding nature of LOI (binding only on exclusivity and confidentiality)
   - Preliminary timeline to closing
2. Obtain board resolution authorizing execution of the LOI
3. Notify NCUA Regional Director of intent to merge — this is a required step before formal application; request pre-application meeting
4. Engage external merger counsel and CPA firm for due diligence support
5. Establish joint due diligence steering committee with target leadership

Confidentiality requirements:
- LOI is CONFIDENTIAL until joint public announcement is planned
- No disclosure to staff beyond CEO, CFO, Compliance Officer, and board
- Document all communications in secure merger file with restricted access

Timeline: LOI executed within 30 days of board authorization
NCUA pre-application meeting: Schedule within 15 days of LOI execution
```

### Step 3: Due Diligence — Financial (CFO + Internal Auditor)

```
Activate CFO and Internal Auditor for financial due diligence on merger target.

Target: [Name] | Due diligence period: [Start date] – [End date]
Data room access: [Location / Virtual platform]

CFO deliverables:
1. Analyze 5 years of audited financial statements:
   - Loan portfolio credit quality: delinquency aging, charge-off history, loss reserve adequacy
   - Investment portfolio: fair value, duration, any OTTI (other-than-temporary impairment)
   - Deposit stability: share certificate concentration, wholesale funding reliance, core deposit ratio
   - Interest rate risk: repricing gap analysis, NII sensitivity model
2. Identify any deferred losses or unrealized impairments not visible in call report data
3. Assess member demographics: age distribution, product penetration, geographic concentration
4. Model purchase accounting adjustments (if applicable under GAAP)
5. Build combined institution pro forma: 3-year income, net worth, and capital projections
6. Identify any contingent liabilities: pending litigation, regulatory actions, off-balance-sheet obligations

Internal Auditor deliverables:
1. Review last 3 years of target's internal and external audit reports
2. Assess internal control environment: segregation of duties, IT controls, cash controls
3. Identify open audit findings and management responses
4. Review vendor contracts: terms, termination provisions, change-of-control clauses
5. Assess operational risk: concentration risks, key-person dependencies, disaster recovery posture

Red flags requiring escalation to CEO before proceeding:
- Net worth below 6% (undercapitalized) with no credible recovery plan
- Loan loss reserves materially understated vs. peer benchmarks
- Unexplained variance in call report data vs. audited financials
- Multiple prior-period adjustments or auditor changes

Timeline: Financial due diligence complete within 60 days of data room access
```

### Step 4: Due Diligence — Compliance & Legal (Compliance Officer + BSA Officer)

```
Activate Compliance Officer and BSA Officer for regulatory and legal due diligence.

Target: [Name] | Due diligence period: [Start date] – [End date]

Compliance Officer deliverables:
1. Review last 3 NCUA examination reports: identify any open findings, MRAs, or enforcement actions
2. Review state regulator exam history (if state-chartered)
3. Assess regulatory compliance program quality:
   - Fair lending: HMDA data analysis, CRA (if applicable), fair lending audit results
   - Consumer compliance: TILA, ECOA, RESPA, FDCPA, TCPA complaint history
   - Privacy: GLB Act compliance, data breach history, member notification record
4. Review all pending and threatened litigation; obtain general counsel summary
5. Review all material contracts for change-of-control provisions requiring counterparty consent
6. Identify any regulatory consent orders, memoranda of understanding (MOUs), or informal agreements
7. Assess member complaint volume and resolution quality (last 2 years)

BSA Officer deliverables:
1. Review BSA/AML program documentation:
   - Written BSA policy and procedures
   - Customer Identification Program (CIP) quality
   - Suspicious Activity Report (SAR) filing history and quality
   - Currency Transaction Report (CTR) volume and accuracy
2. Assess quality and completeness of OFAC screening program
3. Review FinCEN examination or enforcement history
4. Identify any high-risk accounts or MSB relationships that will transfer to us on closing
5. Evaluate BSA staffing and training adequacy at target

Compliance flags:
- Open MRA or consent order → cannot close until resolved or NCUA explicitly approves merger despite open action
- BSA program deficiency → requires remediation plan before closing; we inherit BSA risk
- Pending fair lending investigation → must disclose to NCUA in merger application

Timeline: Compliance due diligence complete within 60 days of data room access
```

### Step 5: Due Diligence — Technology (CTO + Core Systems Administrator)

```
Activate CTO and Core Systems Administrator for technology due diligence.

Target: [Name] | Core system: [System name + version] | Online banking platform: [Name]
Integration complexity: [High / Medium / Low based on core systems compatibility]

CTO deliverables:
1. Assess core processing system: vendor, version, conversion complexity, and timeline
2. Evaluate technology infrastructure: server age, network architecture, cloud vs. on-premise
3. Review cybersecurity posture:
   - Most recent penetration test results
   - Vulnerability management program
   - Security incident history (last 3 years)
   - Multi-factor authentication deployment
4. Assess data quality: member records completeness, duplicate account rate, field mapping compatibility with our core system
5. Identify all data integration points: payroll, collections, card processing, loan origination
6. Estimate technology conversion timeline and cost; include in CFO pro forma

Core Systems Administrator deliverables:
1. Map target's account structure to our core system's account structure
2. Identify data cleansing requirements before conversion
3. Evaluate vendor contracts: core system, card processor, online banking, mobile — change-of-control and conversion cost implications
4. Draft technology conversion project plan with milestones from closing to go-live
5. Identify member-facing services that will be affected during conversion window (plan for zero downtime or clearly communicated maintenance window)

Red flags:
- Core system version is end-of-life and not supported by vendor
- Cybersecurity incident in last 24 months not fully remediated
- Member data quality so poor that conversion timeline exceeds 12 months

Timeline: Technology due diligence complete within 45 days of data room access
```

### Step 6: Regulatory Pre-Application Meeting (CEO + Compliance Officer)

```
Activate CEO and Compliance Officer for NCUA pre-application meeting.

Target: [Name] | NCUA Regional Director: [Name] | Meeting date: [Date]
NCUA notification letter sent: [Date]

Deliverables:
1. Prepare pre-application meeting briefing package:
   - Combined institution overview: asset size, membership, geographic footprint
   - Strategic rationale for merger
   - Financial summary: combined net worth, pro forma projections
   - Identified risks and mitigation plan from due diligence
   - Proposed timeline and member vote plan
2. Attend NCUA pre-application meeting; document all feedback and requirements
3. If target is state-chartered: schedule parallel meeting with state regulator
4. Incorporate NCUA feedback into merger application structure before filing
5. Confirm NCUA's expected application review timeline (typically 90–120 days)

Key questions for NCUA meeting:
- Are there any concerns about the combined institution's FOM?
- What financial condition thresholds does NCUA expect at closing?
- Are there any conditions NCUA anticipates attaching to approval?
- What is the member disclosure package review process?

Documentation: Complete meeting notes; distribute to CEO, CFO, Compliance Officer, and board chair only
Timeline: Meeting scheduled within 30 days of LOI execution; no later than 60 days before planned application filing
```

### Step 7: NCUA Merger Application (Compliance Officer + CEO)

```
Activate Compliance Officer and CEO for NCUA Part 708b merger application filing.

Target: [Name] | Surviving charter: [Our credit union name]
Application deadline (to meet planned closing): [Date — must be ≥ 120 days before closing]

Compliance Officer deliverables:
1. Complete NCUA merger application package (Part 708b):
   - Board resolutions from both credit unions authorizing the merger
   - Executed merger agreement
   - Combined financial statements and pro forma projections
   - FOM analysis: all target members qualify under our charter or FOM amendment filed concurrently
   - Management plan: post-merger leadership structure
   - Technology conversion plan summary
   - Member notification and vote plan (must include 35-day notice period, NCUA Part 708b.8)
   - Member disclosure: financial statements of both CUs as of most recent fiscal year
2. File application with NCUA Regional Office; obtain confirmation of receipt
3. File concurrent state regulator application (state-chartered institutions)
4. Track NCUA review timeline; respond to any requests for additional information within 10 business days

CEO deliverables:
1. Prepare member disclosure package for NCUA review before distribution
2. Ensure merger agreement is signed by both boards and executed counterparts are in file
3. Monitor NCUA review status; maintain weekly contact with NCUA project officer

NCUA member disclosure requirements (708b.8):
- Financial statements of both credit unions
- Description of reason for merger
- Description of how merger will affect member accounts and services
- Vote instructions and deadline
- Must be mailed ≥ 35 days before member vote

Timeline: Application filed within 30 days of completing due diligence; NCUA approval expected 90–120 days after filing
```

### Step 8: Member Communication & Vote (Marketing Manager + CEO)

```
Activate Marketing Manager and CEO for member vote campaign.

Pre-condition: NCUA approval received | Member vote date: [Date]
35-day notice period start: [Date — must be exactly 35 days before vote]

CEO deliverables:
1. Draft member communication package (NCUA-reviewed):
   - Plain-language explanation of why the merger is in members' best interest
   - What changes members will experience: branch access, products, technology
   - What will NOT change: insured status, account numbers (or timeline for any changes)
   - Voting instructions: in-person at annual meeting / mail ballot / electronic ballot
2. Host member town hall meetings (minimum one; more in target service area)
3. Prepare Q&A document for staff answering anticipated member questions

Marketing Manager deliverables:
1. Execute 35-day member notice campaign:
   - Mail member disclosure package (USPS first class — required for regulatory compliance)
   - Email notice to all members with valid email addresses
   - Branch signage and lobby messaging
   - Town hall event invitations (email, direct mail, social media)
2. Staff all branches with talking points; train branch staff on consistent messaging
3. Post merger FAQ on website and mobile app notification

Vote mechanics:
- [ ] Quorum requirement confirmed (per bylaws)
- [ ] Ballot design reviewed by Compliance Officer
- [ ] Independent inspector of election appointed
- [ ] Results certified and documented in board minutes

Timeline: All communications launched exactly on Day 1 of 35-day notice period
```

### Step 9: Conversion Planning (CTO + Core Systems Administrator + Deposit Operations Manager)

```
Activate CTO, Core Systems Administrator, and Deposit Operations Manager for systems conversion planning.

Closing date: [Date] | Core conversion date: [Date — typically 60–90 days post-closing]
Target core system: [Name] | Our core system: [Name]
Core conversion vendor project manager: [Name + contact]

CTO deliverables:
1. Execute data conversion project plan:
   - Data extract from target core system (parallel environment)
   - Data cleansing and field mapping (Core Systems Administrator lead)
   - Test conversion — run 3 full test passes before live conversion
   - User acceptance testing (UAT) by Deposit Operations and Loan Operations staff
2. Manage cutover window: target Friday 6pm – Sunday 6am; communicate to all members in advance
3. Confirm all third-party integrations updated (card processor, ACH, online banking, mobile)
4. Establish Day 1 rollback plan: documented criteria for converting back to interim platform if critical failure

Core Systems Administrator deliverables:
1. Lead data mapping project: account types, product codes, interest rate mapping, officer codes
2. Validate converted data: balance reconciliation (converted balances must match target system within $0.01)
3. Set up converted accounts in our core: maintain target account numbers during transition if feasible to reduce member friction
4. Train target credit union staff on our core system (minimum 8-hour classroom + 4-hour sandbox practice)

Deposit Operations Manager deliverables:
1. Ensure share certificate, IRA, and escrow accounts convert accurately
2. Validate NCUA share insurance coverage for each converted account type
3. Communicate to members whose balances may exceed $250K per-ownership-category coverage after combining with existing accounts
4. Order new debit cards for target members (if card processors are being consolidated)

Timeline: Conversion weekend confirmed ≥ 60 days post-closing; full test complete 2 weeks before live conversion
```

### Step 10: Post-Merger Integration (CEO + All Agents)

```
Activate CEO and all agents for post-merger integration management.

Closing date: [Date] | Core conversion complete: [Date]
Integration tracking dashboard: [Location/system]

CEO deliverables:
1. Establish 90-day integration task force with weekly status meetings
2. Monitor member retention at former target branches: target < 5% membership attrition in first 90 days
3. Communicate combined institution vision to all staff of both legacy organizations
4. Present 90-day and 12-month integration results to board

Agent-specific integration responsibilities:
CFO:
- Reconcile combined balance sheet within 30 days of closing
- File first combined call report accurately (NCUA 5300)
- Confirm all purchase accounting entries recorded correctly

Compliance Officer:
- Notify all regulators of completed merger; confirm licenses and registrations reflect combined entity
- Update all compliance program documents to cover target's former operations
- File updated BSA program certification with FinCEN

BSA Officer:
- Complete BSA risk assessment for combined institution within 60 days of closing
- Review and re-risk-rate any high-risk accounts inherited from target

CHRO:
- Execute staff integration plan: org chart finalized, titles standardized, pay equity review
- Complete benefits harmonization for target staff within 90 days
- Address any staff reductions with documented severance process

Marketing Manager:
- Welcome communication to all converted members: Day 1, Day 30, Day 90 touchpoints
- Update all marketing materials, signage, and web/social presence to reflect combined brand

Timeline: Full integration milestone report to board at 12 months post-closing
```

## Quality Gate Checklist

| # | Criterion | Threshold | Evidence Required | Status |
|---|-----------|-----------|-------------------|--------|
| 1 | NCUA Part 708b merger application approved in writing | 100% — merger cannot close without | NCUA approval letter on file | ☐ |
| 2 | Member vote held with ≥ 35-day advance notice | 100% | Certified ballot results and inspector certification | ☐ |
| 3 | Member disclosure package mailed per 708b.8 requirements | 100% | Mailing certification with postmark | ☐ |
| 4 | Financial due diligence: no undisclosed material liabilities | 100% | Signed due diligence report by CFO + Internal Auditor | ☐ |
| 5 | BSA/AML due diligence complete; no inherited enforcement actions | 100% | BSA Officer sign-off with risk assessment | ☐ |
| 6 | Core conversion: balance reconciliation within $0.01 | 100% | Conversion reconciliation report signed by Core Systems Admin | ☐ |
| 7 | All regulatory notifications filed (NCUA, state, FinCEN) | 100% | Filing confirmations on file | ☐ |
| 8 | 90-day member retention ≥ 95% at converted branches | ≥ 95% | Membership report vs. closing-date baseline | ☐ |

**Gate Keepers**: CEO (strategic and operational) + Compliance Officer (regulatory) — dual sign-off required at closing and at 90-day post-merger review

**Gate Decision**:
- **CLOSE**: All regulatory approvals received, member vote passed, conversion tested — authorized to close and convert
- **DELAY CLOSING**: Open items identified (e.g., NCUA condition unmet, conversion test failed) — CEO and Compliance Officer determine resolution timeline
- **ABORT**: Material due diligence finding, NCUA denial, or member vote failure — CEO presents findings to board; document basis for merger termination
