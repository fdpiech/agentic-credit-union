# Workflow G Playbook — Annual Strategic Planning

> **Cycle Time**: August–December (5-month cycle) | **Agents**: 9 | **Gate Keepers**: CEO + Board of Directors

---

## Objective

Develop and execute a 3-year strategic plan and annual operating budget that positions the credit union for sustainable growth, sound capitalization, and mission fulfillment. Every strategic priority must be financially feasible, organizationally supported, and board-approved before the fiscal year begins.

## Pre-Conditions

- [ ] Prior year strategic plan performance data compiled (CEO)
- [ ] NCUA call report peer comparison data available (CFO)
- [ ] Board strategic planning calendar confirmed (CEO + Board Chair)
- [ ] Environmental scan request distributed to all functional leaders (August 1)

## Regulatory Framework

| Area | Requirement | Owner |
|------|------------|-------|
| Capital planning | Net worth ratio ≥ 7% (Well Capitalized per NCUA Part 702) | CFO |
| ALCO policy | Board-approved Asset/Liability policy with rate risk limits | CFO + Risk Manager |
| Strategic plan | Board-adopted written strategic plan (supervisory expectation) | CEO |
| Budget approval | Board approval before fiscal year start | CEO + CFO |
| Information security plan | Board-approved annually (NCUA Part 748) | Core Systems Administrator |
| BSA program | Board-approved BSA compliance program (annual review) | BSA Officer |

## Stage 1: Environmental Scan (August 1–September 15)

### Step 1A: CEO Commissions Environmental Scan

```
Activate Credit Union CEO to launch the annual strategic planning process.

Planning year: [YEAR] | Plan horizon: [3-year period]
Last strategic plan adopted: [Date]
Board strategic planning session scheduled: [Date]

Deliverables required:
1. Distribute environmental scan templates to all functional leaders with August 31 deadline
2. Schedule executive team strategy retreat (September, 1 day off-site)
3. Request peer group benchmarking data from CFO (NCUA call report comparison)
4. Commission member satisfaction analysis from Marketing Manager
5. Review prior year strategic plan — which priorities were achieved, which fell short?

Scan areas to commission:
- CFO: Financial performance vs. peers, capital adequacy, rate environment
- Compliance Officer: Regulatory horizon (24-month outlook)
- Risk Manager: Enterprise risk landscape changes
- Marketing Manager: Member satisfaction data, competitive environment
- Core Systems Administrator: Technology landscape, vendor assessments
- Branch Manager: Front-line member and staff feedback
- CHRO: Talent and workforce trends
- Internal Auditor: Audit insights and governance observations

Timeline: Scan requests out by August 1; responses due August 31
```

### Step 1B: CFO Peer Group Financial Benchmarking

```
Activate CFO for annual peer group financial benchmarking analysis.

Peer group: [NCUA-defined peer group by asset size or state]
Data source: NCUA Call Report (most recent 12-month period)

Deliverables required:
1. Net worth ratio — CU vs. peer median and 25th/75th percentiles
2. Return on assets (ROA) — CU vs. peer
3. Net interest margin (NIM) — CU vs. peer and trend
4. Efficiency ratio — CU vs. peer
5. Loan-to-share ratio — CU vs. peer
6. Delinquency ratio (60+ days) — CU vs. peer
7. Net charge-off rate — CU vs. peer
8. Loan portfolio composition comparison
9. Deposit/share composition comparison
10. Salary and benefit expense as % of assets — peer comparison

Output: Executive-level benchmarking summary with 3–5 strategic implications
Timeline: Complete by August 31
```

### Step 1C: Compliance Officer Regulatory Horizon Assessment

```
Activate Compliance Officer for 24-month regulatory horizon assessment.

Period: [Current month] through [24 months forward]

Deliverables required:
1. Pending NCUA rulemakings that will affect operations:
   - Rule name, effective date, operational impact, preparation required
2. CFPB proposed or final rules affecting credit union products
3. FinCEN/BSA regulatory changes (beneficial ownership updates, AML modernization)
4. State law changes (if state-chartered)
5. NCUA examination trend report — what are examiners focusing on nationally?
6. Fair lending environment — CFPB and DOJ enforcement trends
7. Data privacy regulatory developments (state laws, federal proposals)

Output: Regulatory horizon report with timeline and preparation requirements
Strategic implication: Which regulatory changes require capital, staffing, or system investment?
Timeline: Complete by August 31
```

### Step 1D: Risk Manager Enterprise Risk Landscape

```
Activate Risk Manager for annual enterprise risk landscape assessment.

Assessment period: Prior 12 months | Horizon: 24 months forward

Deliverables required:
1. Credit risk: Portfolio quality trend, concentration risks, economic outlook impact
2. Interest rate risk: Current rate sensitivity position vs. policy limits; rate environment outlook
3. Liquidity risk: Funding diversification, deposit concentration, borrowing capacity
4. Operational risk: Significant operational events in prior year; emerging risks
5. Third-party/vendor risk: Critical vendor health and dependency assessment
6. Cyber/information security risk: Threat landscape changes
7. Strategic risk: Risks inherent in proposed strategic direction
8. Top 5 enterprise risks for next planning horizon with likelihood and impact ratings

Output: Enterprise risk landscape report with top 5 risks and recommended risk appetite statements
Timeline: Complete by August 31
```

### Step 1E: Marketing Manager Member Analysis

```
Activate Marketing Manager for annual member satisfaction and competitive analysis.

Data sources: [Member survey, NPS results, complaint data, product usage analytics]

Deliverables required:
1. Member satisfaction scores (overall and by product/channel) vs. prior year
2. Net Promoter Score with trend
3. New member acquisition: volume, channels, demographics, eligibility basis
4. Member attrition: count, reasons (exit surveys), products held at departure
5. Product penetration rates: products per member, digital adoption, loan product usage
6. Competitive landscape: significant moves by banks or fintechs in the market
7. Member demographic trends: age, income, geography — where is the base going?
8. Voice of the Member: top 3-5 themes from complaints, surveys, and branch feedback

Output: Member intelligence report with strategic growth opportunities identified
Timeline: Complete by August 31
```

## Stage 2: Strategic Prioritization (September 15–October 31)

### Step 2A: Executive Strategy Retreat (CEO facilitates)

```
Activate Credit Union CEO to facilitate executive team strategy retreat.

Input: All environmental scan deliverables from Stage 1
Participants: CEO, CFO, Branch Manager, CHRO, Compliance Officer, Risk Manager, Core Systems Administrator

Retreat agenda:
1. Performance review: Prior year strategic plan scorecard (1 hour)
2. Environmental scan synthesis: Each leader presents key findings (90 minutes)
3. SWOT analysis: Synthesize into Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats (1 hour)
4. Strategic priority brainstorm: Generate candidate strategic priorities (1 hour)
5. Prioritization: Score each candidate by Impact, Feasibility, Urgency, and Mission alignment
6. Draft 3-5 strategic priorities with 3-year outcome definitions (1 hour)

Output: Draft strategic priorities with 3-year outcome definitions
CEO deliverable: Written SWOT analysis + draft strategic priority list within 5 days of retreat
```

### Step 2B: CFO Financial Feasibility Modeling

```
Activate CFO for financial feasibility analysis of draft strategic priorities.

Input: Draft strategic priorities from Step 2A

For each proposed strategic priority, model:
1. Capital requirement: Investment needed and source (earnings vs. external)
2. Revenue impact: Projected revenue contribution at 1-year and 3-year marks
3. Expense impact: Operating expense additions or reductions
4. Net worth ratio impact: Pro forma net worth under each priority
5. Staffing impact: FTE additions or reductions
6. Break-even timeline: When does the initiative become net-positive?
7. Risk scenario: Downside case if initiative underperforms by 50%

Output: Financial feasibility matrix for each proposed priority
Recommendation: Which priorities are financially sustainable vs. require phasing?
Timeline: 2 weeks after draft priorities are received
```

### Step 2C: CHRO Talent Capacity Analysis

```
Activate CHRO for organizational capacity assessment of strategic priorities.

Input: Draft strategic priorities from Step 2A + CFO staffing impact from Step 2B

Deliverables required:
1. Current talent inventory: Skills and capacity gaps vs. proposed strategic direction
2. Hiring plan: Positions needed, timeline, budget estimate, local market availability
3. Development plan: Internal talent development requirements for new direction
4. Culture assessment: Does proposed strategy require culture shift? What does that take?
5. Succession risks: Key person dependencies in proposed strategic execution
6. Total compensation impact: Compensation program adjustments needed to attract/retain

Output: Human capital plan aligned to proposed strategic priorities
Timeline: 2 weeks after draft priorities received
```

### Step 2D: CEO Revises and Finalizes Strategic Priorities

```
Activate Credit Union CEO to finalize strategic priorities for board presentation.

Input: Feasibility analysis (CFO), talent plan (CHRO), regulatory scan (Compliance), risk landscape (Risk Manager)

Deliverables required:
1. Final 3-5 strategic priorities with:
   - 3-year outcome definition (what does success look like in 3 years?)
   - Year 1 milestones (what must be accomplished this year?)
   - Lead accountable leader
   - Financial investment required (from CFO model)
   - Key risk (from Risk Manager)
   - Regulatory consideration (from Compliance Officer)
2. Draft strategic narrative (1-2 pages) for board presentation
3. Connection to cooperative values and member mission

Output: Draft strategic plan document for senior team review
Timeline: November 1 target for board presentation
```

## Stage 3: Annual Operating Plan (October 15–November 30)

### Step 3A: CFO Drafts Annual Budget

```
Activate CFO to develop the annual operating budget aligned to strategic priorities.

Input: Approved strategic priorities, peer benchmarking data, rate environment outlook

Budget components:
1. Income Statement projection:
   - Loan interest income (by portfolio segment with growth assumptions)
   - Investment income
   - Fee income (by product)
   - Non-interest expense (by department — request from each functional leader)
   - Provision for loan losses
   - Net income / ROA target

2. Balance Sheet projection:
   - Loan portfolio growth by segment (lending input)
   - Share/deposit growth by product (deposit ops input)
   - Investment portfolio changes (ALCO-driven)
   - Capital adequacy — beginning and end-of-year net worth ratio

3. Key ratio targets:
   - Net worth ratio: ≥ 7.0% (Well Capitalized) minimum; target [X]%
   - ROA target
   - Efficiency ratio target
   - NIM target vs. rate environment

4. Strategic investment line items: Budget for each approved strategic priority
5. Rate scenario analysis: Budget under +200bps, flat, -100bps rate scenarios

Output: Draft annual budget with supporting schedules
Timeline: Draft to CEO by November 15; board presentation by November 30
```

### Step 3B: Each Functional Leader Submits Departmental Goals

```
Activate [EACH FUNCTIONAL LEADER] for departmental goal submission.

Leaders: Branch Manager, Compliance Officer, BSA Officer, CHRO, Core Systems Administrator,
         Marketing Manager, Risk Manager, Internal Auditor

Deliverables per leader:
1. 3-5 departmental goals aligned to the approved strategic priorities
2. Resource requirements (FTE, budget, technology)
3. Key metrics and measurement approach
4. Dependencies (what does this department need from others?)
5. Timeline and milestones

Output: Departmental goal worksheet submitted to CEO and CFO
Timeline: November 10 deadline
```

### Step 3C: Risk Manager Budget Stress Test

```
Activate Risk Manager for annual budget stress test.

Input: Draft annual budget from Step 3A

Stress scenarios to model:
1. Recession scenario: 25% loan volume reduction, delinquency doubles, NIM compresses 50bps
   - Net worth ratio impact
   - Provision expense impact
   - Action plan if this scenario materializes

2. Rate shock scenario: +300bps over 12 months
   - NIM impact (short-term vs. long-term assets)
   - Deposit repricing behavior assumption
   - Investment portfolio unrealized loss impact
   - Net worth ratio impact

3. Operational stress: Loss of key vendor + major operational event in same year
   - Revenue impact
   - Remediation cost estimate

4. Strategic initiative failure: Largest proposed initiative generates 50% of projected revenue
   - Net worth impact
   - Go/no-go decision point

Output: Budget stress test report with risk appetite conclusion
Recommendation: Is the proposed budget within the board-approved risk appetite?
Timeline: Complete by November 20
```

## Stage 4: Board Adoption (November–December)

### Step 4A: CEO Board Strategic Planning Session

```
Activate Credit Union CEO for board strategic planning presentation.

Board session format: [Retreat / Extended board meeting] | Date: [Date]
Audience: Board of Directors + optional senior leadership participation

Presentation agenda:
1. Environmental scan summary: Key findings from each functional area (20 min)
2. Prior year strategic plan scorecard: What we achieved, what we missed, why (15 min)
3. SWOT analysis: Board discussion of strategic landscape (20 min)
4. Proposed strategic priorities: Present each priority with 3-year outcomes (30 min)
5. Board discussion and input (30 min)
6. CEO summarizes board direction for final plan refinement

CEO deliverable: Revised final strategic plan incorporating board input within 2 weeks of session
```

### Step 4B: CFO Board Budget Presentation

```
Activate CFO for board budget presentation.

Board meeting: [Date — December]
Budget materials required for distribution ≥5 days before meeting:
1. Income statement budget with prior year actuals and current year projection
2. Balance sheet budget with growth assumptions
3. Key financial ratios vs. prior year and peer group
4. Capital adequacy projection (net worth ratio at year-end)
5. Strategic investment budget summary with expected returns
6. Rate scenario analysis (base, up 200, down 100)
7. Stress test summary (Risk Manager input)

Board discussion points:
- Does the budget achieve the financial targets aligned with strategy?
- Is the capital plan adequate? Any capital restoration concerns?
- Are strategic investments funded at adequate levels?
- Are the assumptions realistic given the economic environment?

Board action required: Approve operating budget by resolution
Compliance note: Budget approval must be documented in board meeting minutes
```

### Step 4C: CEO Communicates Plan to All Staff

```
Activate Credit Union CEO for all-staff strategic plan communication.

Timeline: Within 2 weeks of board adoption

Communication deliverables:
1. All-staff message from CEO: Strategic priorities in plain language
   - Why these priorities? (Connect to member mission and cooperative values)
   - What does success look like in 3 years?
   - How does each employee's work connect to the plan?
2. Department-level briefings by each functional leader (coordinated by CHRO)
3. Strategic plan summary document: 1-2 pages, shareable with new employees
4. FAQ document for common staff questions

CHRO concurrent action:
- Align individual performance goals to strategic priorities for management staff
- Update performance review templates to include strategic plan contribution section
- Communicate new performance expectations to all managers
```

## Stage 5: Execution and Monitoring (Ongoing)

### Monthly Executive Dashboard (CEO)

```
Activate Credit Union CEO for monthly strategic plan monitoring.

Dashboard elements — compile by 15th of each month:
1. Strategic priority scorecard: Each priority with milestone status (On Track / At Risk / Off Track)
2. Financial performance: Actual vs. budget — key ratios only (ROA, NIM, NWR, Delinquency)
3. Member metrics: Net membership growth, products per member, NPS (quarterly)
4. Risk indicators: Any risk escalations from Risk Manager or Compliance Officer
5. Action items from prior month: What was committed, what was delivered?

Report to: Board of Directors (monthly summary), senior leadership team (full dashboard)
Format: 1-page executive summary + supporting data appendix
```

### Quarterly Board Progress Report (CEO)

```
Activate Credit Union CEO for quarterly strategic plan board report.

Quarterly report components:
1. Strategic plan progress: Each priority — milestones achieved, milestones missed, revised outlook
2. Financial performance vs. budget: Income statement, balance sheet, key ratios
3. Risk environment update: Any material changes since last quarter
4. Competitive and regulatory update: Significant market or regulatory developments
5. Capital position: Net worth ratio vs. Well Capitalized threshold and board target
6. Next quarter priorities: What the team is focusing on in the next 90 days

Board action required: Acknowledge report; approve any material changes to strategic direction
```

### Mid-Year Strategy Review (CEO — July)

```
Activate Credit Union CEO for mid-year strategy review.

Trigger: July board meeting (or off-cycle if environment changes materially)

Review questions:
1. Are we on track for each strategic priority? What's the adjusted forecast for year-end?
2. Has the environment changed materially since we adopted the plan?
   (Rate environment, competitive landscape, regulatory, economic conditions)
3. Do any priorities need to be accelerated, modified, or deferred?
4. Are financial results tracking to budget? If not, what are the implications?
5. Are there any capital adequacy concerns emerging?

Possible actions:
- Reaffirm plan as approved
- Modify specific initiative timelines or targets (board notification)
- Defer a strategic initiative to protect capital (board approval required)
- Accelerate a priority based on market opportunity (board approval if material)
```

## Quality Gate Checklist

| # | Criterion | Threshold | Evidence Required | Status |
|---|-----------|-----------|-------------------|--------|
| 1 | Environmental scan completed by all functional leaders | 100% of assigned leaders | Completed scan documents on file | ☐ |
| 2 | Financial feasibility modeled for each strategic priority | 100% of proposed priorities | CFO financial model with pro forma ratios | ☐ |
| 3 | Risk stress test completed on proposed budget | Annual | Risk Manager stress test report | ☐ |
| 4 | CHRO talent capacity assessment completed | Annual | Human capital plan on file | ☐ |
| 5 | Board strategic planning session held | Annual | Board meeting minutes with strategic discussion documented | ☐ |
| 6 | Strategic plan adopted by board resolution | By December 31 | Board resolution in meeting minutes | ☐ |
| 7 | Annual budget approved by board before fiscal year start | By December 31 | Board approval resolution in minutes | ☐ |
| 8 | Strategic plan communicated to all staff | Within 2 weeks of board adoption | Communication log; CHRO confirmation | ☐ |
| 9 | Individual management goals aligned to strategic plan | 100% of management staff by January 31 | Signed performance agreements | ☐ |
| 10 | Net worth ratio projected to remain Well Capitalized | ≥ 7.0% at year-end under base scenario | CFO capital plan with year-end projection | ☐ |

**Gate Keeper**: CEO + Board of Directors

**Gate Decision**:
- **COMPLETE**: Board has adopted plan and budget; staff communication delivered; goals set
- **INCOMPLETE**: Missing board adoption or budget approval — escalate to Board Chair immediately
- **BLOCKED**: Capital concern identified — defer discretionary strategic investments until resolved
